# Meeting Follow-Up — Templates and Cadence

> Last verified: May 2026. Templates and cadence based on practitioner experience across KSA, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, and Oman.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

The single largest preventable cause of lost deals in the GCC is **bad follow-up**. Two specific failure modes account for ~80% of losses:

1. **Following up too fast or too aggressively** (within hours; or 4+ touches in the first week). Signals desperation. Burns relationships.
2. **Not following up at all after the first thank-you.** GCC buyers don't typically chase. If you go silent after 1 week, the deal is dead even if they liked you.

The right cadence: thank-you within 24 hours, gentle nudge at 1 week, value-add at 3 weeks, calendar request at 6 weeks, event re-engagement at 3 months. Drop off only after that, not before.

This file gives you the operational playbook.

## The follow-up cadence (default)

### T+0 (within 24 hours): the thank-you
- **Channel:** email (LinkedIn is acceptable for casual / event-met contacts).
- **Length:** 80–120 words.
- **Purpose:** acknowledge the meeting, deliver any promised material, confirm next step.

### T+1 week: the gentle nudge
- **Channel:** email.
- **Length:** 60–100 words.
- **Purpose:** check in on the proposed next step.
- **Tone:** patient, not pushing. Acknowledge they may be busy.

### T+3 weeks: the value-add
- **Channel:** email.
- **Length:** 80–120 words.
- **Purpose:** share something genuinely useful (industry report, article, intro to relevant party). Not a sales push.

### T+6 weeks: the calendar request
- **Channel:** email + optional WhatsApp ping if relationship allows.
- **Length:** 60–80 words.
- **Purpose:** ask for a specific 30-minute call or in-person meeting next month.

### T+3 months: event re-engagement
- **Channel:** email or LinkedIn.
- **Purpose:** suggest meeting at an upcoming event (LEAP / GITEX / Web Summit Qatar / etc.).

### After T+3 months
- Move to quarterly outreach. Holiday greetings (Ramadan, Eid, National Day) are appropriate touchpoints. Don't push for meetings unless they re-engage.

## Templates

### T+0: Thank-you (after first meeting)

**Subject:** Thank you — [topic of conversation]

```
Dear [First Name],

Thank you for the time today. I particularly appreciated the conversation 
about [specific topic they raised — ideally something they said in their 
own words].

As promised, attaching/sharing:
1. [Material 1 — e.g., "Our 2-page summary of the European municipal 
   deployment we discussed."]
2. [Material 2 — e.g., "The technical specification you asked about."]
3. [Action you committed to — e.g., "I'll introduce you to [Name] by 
   end of week."]

For next step: [specific named action with date — e.g., "We'd like to 
schedule a technical deep-dive with your engineering team in the week 
of [specific date]. Suggested times: Tuesday 10am or Wednesday 2pm 
GCC time."]

Thank you again — and looking forward to continuing the conversation.

With best regards,
[Your Name]
[Title, Company]
[Phone with country code / WhatsApp]
[LinkedIn]
```

### T+0 alternate: After a senior or ceremonial first meeting

For royal-family, ministerial, or family-principal meetings.

**Subject:** With gratitude — [Specific topic or vision pillar reference]

```
Your Excellency / Dear [Sheikh / Sheikha / Title and Name],

Thank you for the honor of your time today. The conversation about 
[specific topic] was both insightful and inspiring — and your perspective 
on [specific point they raised] gave us much to consider.

As discussed, I am sharing [material]. We remain committed to [specific 
alignment — e.g., "supporting the realization of Vision 2030's Smart 
Cities pillar in any way our work can contribute."]

If a follow-up briefing with your team would be helpful, I would welcome 
the opportunity at a time of your convenience. I am available [specific 
window].

With deepest gratitude and respect,
[Your Name]
[Title, Company]
[Direct contact]
```

### T+1 week: Gentle nudge

**Subject:** Following up — [original meeting topic]

```
Dear [First Name],

Hope this finds you well. Following up on our conversation last week — 
I wanted to gently check whether you had a chance to review [material 
sent] or to consider the next-step suggestion.

I appreciate you may be in the middle of [seasonal context — e.g., 
"Q4 planning" / "Ramadan schedule" / "summer travel"]. Whenever it's 
convenient, I'd value 30 minutes to continue the conversation.

Suggested times: [specific 3 options across 2 weeks].

With best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### T+3 weeks: Value-add

**Subject:** Thought you might find this useful — [specific topic]

```
Dear [First Name],

Hope you've had a productive few weeks. I came across [specific 
resource — "the recent ATKearney report on smart-water in KSA" / 
"a McKinsey piece on UAE fintech consolidation" / "an interview with 
the Minister of Industry"] and thought of our conversation about 
[their topic].

Sharing in case useful — no reply needed.

Separately, when the time is right, I'd still welcome a chance to 
continue our discussion. I'll be in [city] during [specific window].

With best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### T+6 weeks: Calendar request

**Subject:** Suggestion for next month — [city / event]

```
Dear [First Name],

It's been a few weeks since we last spoke. I'm planning a visit to 
[city] during [specific dates 2–4 weeks out] and would value the 
opportunity to continue our conversation about [original topic].

Even 30 minutes over coffee would be useful. Suggested times: 
[3 specific options across the visit period].

If the visit isn't ideal, a video call in the same window also works.

With best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### T+3 months: Event re-engagement

**Subject:** [Event name] — meeting up?

```
Dear [First Name],

I hope this finds you well — and that the year is going strong.

I'll be at [Event name — e.g., LEAP 2027 / GITEX 2026 / Web Summit Qatar / 
Bahrain Fintech Forward] during [specific dates]. Would you be open to a 
brief catch-up while we're both there?

I'd be glad to share what we've been building since our last conversation 
— and to hear how [their organization's specific initiative] has evolved.

With best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Ramadan greeting (sent in the week BEFORE Ramadan starts)

**Subject:** Ramadan Mubarak

```
Dear [First Name],

As we approach the holy month of Ramadan, I wanted to send my warm 
wishes to you and your family for a peaceful and meaningful month ahead.

Ramadan Kareem.

With warm regards,
[Your Name]
```

Send to ALL Muslim contacts. No business content. Critical relationship maintenance moment.

### Eid greeting (Eid al-Fitr — after Ramadan; Eid al-Adha — during Hajj)

**Subject:** Eid Mubarak

```
Dear [First Name],

Wishing you and your family a joyful Eid [al-Fitr / al-Adha] — and 
many blessings for the year ahead.

Eid Mubarak.

With warm regards,
[Your Name]
```

### National Day greeting (country-specific)

For each country's National Day (see `references/culture/sales-cycles-calendar.md`).

**Subject (KSA):** Saudi National Day greetings
```
Dear [First Name],

Wishing you and your colleagues a wonderful Saudi National Day. 
Honoring the Kingdom's progress and its inspiring vision for the future.

With warm regards,
[Your Name]
```

(Adapt for UAE Union Day, Qatar National Day, Bahrain National Day, Kuwait National & Liberation Days, Oman National Day.)

## Channel selection guide

| Situation | Best channel | Why |
|---|---|---|
| First post-meeting thank-you | | Formal, traceable. |
| 1-week nudge | | Slight escalation, still respectful. |
| Calendar coordination, after relationship established | WhatsApp | GCC's primary business messaging app. |
| Senior/ceremonial first contact | | Plus printed letter for very senior contexts. |
| Ramadan / Eid / National Day greeting | + (after relationship established) | Email primary; WhatsApp acceptable once relationship is established. |
| Sharing an article / news / value-add | + LinkedIn | LinkedIn DM works for less senior contacts. |
| Urgent operational question | WhatsApp (if relationship allows) or phone | Speed matters. |
| Confirming a meeting 24h before | WhatsApp | Standard practice. Not pushy. |

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't follow up the same day you met. Wait at least 24 hours.
- ❌ Don't follow up more than once per week in the first month.
- ❌ Don't send a 4-page proposal in the first follow-up. Keep first thank-you short.
- ❌ Don't CC their boss in follow-up unless they explicitly suggested it.
- ❌ Don't use generic "just checking in" subject lines after week 1. Add value or ask specifically.
- ❌ Don't escalate to LinkedIn DM after an email goes unanswered. It reads as pursuit.
- ❌ Don't send marketing newsletters to GCC contacts you've only met once. Permission-based only.
- ❌ Don't sign emails "Best" if you're new to the relationship. Use "With best regards" or "Kind regards."
- ❌ Don't pressure for a decision by date X. GCC enterprise buying is consensus-driven; deadline pressure backfires.
- ❌ Don't use auto-scheduling tools (Calendly, Cal.com) for senior contacts in first interactions. Reads as transactional. Suggest specific times instead.

## Handling specific re-engagement scenarios

### They went silent after meeting 1

- Send T+1 week nudge.
- If no response, send T+3 weeks value-add.
- If no response, send T+6 weeks calendar request.
- If still no response, assume polite "no." Move to T+3 month event re-engagement.

### They responded enthusiastically then went silent

- Often means they ran into internal procurement / budget / political obstacles.
- T+2 weeks: send a "checking in — anything I can help unblock?" email.
- Offer to provide additional materials, references, or to talk to a colleague.

### They keep rescheduling

- Standard pattern in the GCC; respect it.
- Don't express frustration. Re-propose times warmly.
- After 3 reschedules: shift to "no pressure to schedule — let me know when easier" mode.

### A junior contact has gone cold

- Could mean: lost organizational support, left the company, or assigned to other priorities.
- LinkedIn-search to verify they're still in the role.
- If still in role: re-engage gently with value-add content.
- If they've left: research who replaced them and re-engage with the new person.

### A senior contact has gone cold

- Almost certainly: travel, holiday, or competing priorities.
- Wait 2–3 weeks longer than you would in a Western context.
- Re-engage via mutual contact if available.
- Don't push direct multiple times.

## What outside founders should know

- **The follow-up game is multi-month, not multi-week.** A 6-month "warm" engagement with steady value-add is normal for GCC enterprise deals. Western "30-day pipeline urgency" mindset is the wrong calibration.
- **One bad follow-up doesn't always kill a deal — but it adds 2–3 months to closing.** Tone matters more than frequency.
- **WhatsApp is the default channel once relationship is established.** If a senior contact gives you their WhatsApp number, the relationship has crossed a threshold. Treat it with respect — don't spam.
- **Holidays are not dead time.** Ramadan, Eid, and National Day greetings are critical relationship maintenance touchpoints. Skipping them is read as inattention.
- **Always reply to their messages within 24 hours, even if you don't have a substantive answer.** "Will come back to you with detail by Wednesday" is enough. Silence is read as disrespect.

## Verify before sending follow-up

- Spelling of name and title (especially after meetings — easy to mis-hear and mis-spell).
- Specific topic reference is accurate (not paraphrased to the point of seeming inattentive).
- Promised materials are actually attached / linked.
- Tone matches the relationship register (formal for senior; warm-professional for peer).
- Send time is local-appropriate (morning, weekday, not during prayer time).
