# Pitch Deck Localization — GCC Adaptations

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: regional sales-leader interviews, pitch-feedback from Madinah Tech Cultivator cohort and other regional accelerators (Hub71, in5, Sheraa, Sanabil 500).

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

A pitch deck that wins in San Francisco / London / Singapore will land flat in Riyadh / Abu Dhabi / Doha unless you localize it. The five biggest adjustments:

1. **Lead with Vision alignment.** Slide 2 (after the title) should reference the specific Vision pillar (Vision 2030 / We the UAE 2031 / D33 / NDS3 / Vision 2040) and KPI you address. Western decks lead with problem; GCC decks lead with strategic alignment.
2. **Replace US-centric logos and case studies.** Even 2–3 regional logos or relevant case studies dramatically improve credibility. A 100% US-customer deck signals you haven't done the work.
3. **Currency: SAR / AED / QAR primary; USD secondary.** Especially for government / SOE pitches. Use local currency for the primary number, USD in parentheses.
4. **VAT-inclusive vs exclusive must be clear.** KSA 15%, UAE 5%, Bahrain 10%, Oman 5%, Qatar/Kuwait 0%. Procurement teams will flag ambiguity.
5. **Add a "GCC commitment" slide.** RHQ status / planned, ICV stance, Saudization / Emiratisation / Omanisation hiring plan, data residency strategy. This pre-empts procurement objections and signals seriousness.

## The GCC pitch deck structure (15 slides for a 30-minute meeting)

### Slide 1 — Title
- Company name (Arabic transliteration optional but appreciated for KSA / Qatar / Oman audiences).
- Tagline in English (and optionally Arabic).
- Presenter name, title, contact (phone with country code + WhatsApp icon + LinkedIn).
- Meeting date and location (signals attention to detail).

### Slide 2 — Vision alignment ⚠️ (this is the GCC-specific slide that doesn't exist in Western decks)

Specific structure:
- Name the specific Vision pillar (e.g. "Saudi Vision 2030 — Quality of Life Program → Healthcare Transformation").
- Name the specific KPI you address (e.g. "Reduce healthcare wait-times by 60% by 2030").
- One sentence: how your product directly contributes to that KPI.

If your product genuinely doesn't tie to a Vision pillar — your product probably doesn't have GCC market fit. Reconsider before pitching.

### Slide 3 — The problem
- Specific to the GCC context, not a generic global problem.
- Numbers from GASTAT / FCSC / SCAD / PSA / NCSI / CSB / iGA, not from Western consultancy reports.
- The buyer's actual pain point as they would describe it.

### Slide 4 — Why now
- Specific policy / regulatory shift creating urgency (RHQ rule, PDPL, Saudization tightening, Vision-pillar deadline).
- Recent comparable wins (e.g. "the Ministry of X awarded $Y to address this in Q1 2026").

### Slide 5 — The product / solution
- Visual; show, don't tell.
- Use case framed around a GCC scenario (Riyadh metro / Mubadala portfolio company / DEWA pilot / etc.).
- Arabic UI screenshot if you have it — major credibility signal.

### Slide 6 — Why us (team)
- Founder credentials.
- Regional advisor / board / partner — if you have a credible GCC-based advisor, name them here (with permission). Single biggest credibility boost.
- Local presence plan if not yet established.

### Slide 7 — Traction (with regional anchor)
- Total numbers globally.
- Specific GCC traction (pilot, LOI, MoU, signed) called out.
- If pre-traction in GCC: name 3–5 GCC pilots in your pipeline with specific contacts (anonymize if needed).

### Slide 8 — Customer evidence
- 1–2 deep case studies with quantified outcomes.
- At least 1 should be GCC-relevant if you have it; if not, a comparable-context case study from Singapore / Malaysia / Türkiye / UK works better than US.

### Slide 9 — Business model and pricing
- Pricing in local currency primary, USD secondary.
- Specify VAT treatment (inclusive vs exclusive).
- Multi-year discount structure (GCC enterprise buyers expect this).
- Payment terms (60-90 days typical for enterprise; 90-120 for government).

### Slide 10 — Market sizing
- GCC TAM/SAM/SOM bottom-up — see `frameworks/market-sizing.md`.
- Country-by-country split.
- Defensible methodology (cited sources visible).

### Slide 11 — Competitive landscape
- Acknowledge the global incumbents AND the regional incumbents (solutions by stc, Elm, e& enterprise, Core42, BCT, etc.).
- Your differentiation in the GCC context specifically (localization, speed, technical depth, etc.).

### Slide 12 — Go-to-market (GCC-specific)
- Country sequencing (typically KSA + UAE first, then add 1–2 others).
- Soft-landing program engagement (NTDP / Hub71 / Sheraa / Tamkeen / KDIPA / FFO / OPAZ).
- Channel partnership strategy (which SI / which bank / which conglomerate).
- Government engagement strategy (Etimad registration / Tamm / RHQ).

### Slide 13 — GCC commitment (the second GCC-specific slide)

Structure:
- **RHQ status:** plan, timeline, cost commitment (KSA-specific).
- **ICV stance:** approach to local procurement, local hiring, local capex.
- **Localization hiring plan:** Saudization / Emiratisation / Omanisation targets by year.
- **Data residency:** local hosting commitment (AWS KSA 2026, AWS Bahrain, Azure UAE, Microsoft Qatar, Oracle UAE Sovereign).
- **Tax / regulatory:** RHQ tax structure, VAT registration plan, withholding tax handling.

This slide signals to procurement / legal that you've done the work. It pre-empts 80% of the objections that kill deals later.

### Slide 14 — The ask
- Pilot terms (duration, cost, success criteria) OR investment ask OR partnership ask.
- Specific calendar item: "We'd like to scope a 6-week pilot starting [specific month]" or "We're targeting first close [specific date]."
- Clear and bounded.

### Slide 15 — Next step + contact
- Specific call to action.
- Contact details (phone with country code + WhatsApp + email + LinkedIn).
- "Thank you" in Arabic (شكراً — Shukran) appreciated.

## Visual design adjustments

### Fonts
- English: use a font with strong Arabic-script equivalent. **Tajawal**, **Cairo**, **Noto Sans Arabic**, **IBM Plex Sans Arabic** are good free options that have matched Latin + Arabic glyphs.
- Avoid: ornate / serif fonts that don't have Arabic equivalents.

### Right-to-left considerations
- If you produce an Arabic version of the deck: page numbering goes right-to-left.
- Cover slide right-aligned for Arabic deck.
- Charts: bar charts work in either direction; line charts read left-to-right in both English and Arabic versions (this is convention).

### Color palette
- **Conservative blues / greens / golds** read as serious / institutional. Common in GCC government / financial-services contexts.
- **Bright primary colors** read as Western-tech / startup-y. Fine for tech-vertical audiences (G42, Hub71, Sheraa); risky for traditional government / family-conglomerate audiences.
- **Avoid:** Israeli national colors in any context (blue + white in flag configuration); imagery of Israel.
- **Avoid:** Iranian flag colors in Saudi/UAE context.
- **Avoid:** alcohol imagery anywhere.
- **Avoid:** women in revealing clothing in KSA, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar contexts (Bahrain, UAE more flexible).
- **Avoid:** dogs in product imagery for conservative audiences (dogs are considered unclean in Islamic tradition; cats are fine).

### Imagery
- **Use:** GCC city skylines (Riyadh, Dubai, Doha, Manama, Kuwait City, Muscat) if your product relates to those geographies.
- **Use:** images that show diverse but appropriately-attired teams.
- **Avoid:** stock imagery of obviously Western settings (NYC subway, London tube, etc.) unless directly relevant.
- **Avoid:** generic "diverse team" stock photos — they read as inauthentic.

```
$50,000 USD / year
(SAR 187,500 / year, VAT exclusive)
```

```
SAR 187,500 / year (VAT exclusive)
≈ USD 50,000
Plus 15% KSA VAT where applicable
```

```
AED 184,000 / year (VAT exclusive)
≈ USD 50,000
```

## Sample Vision-alignment slide language

### KSA government smart-city pitch

> "Saudi Vision 2030 — Quality of Life Program targets a 100% smart-city infrastructure deployment across the 13 regional capitals by 2030. Our platform addresses two specific KPIs: real-time waste-collection optimization (target: 30% reduction in collection costs) and water-loss reduction (target: 20% reduction by 2030). We have deployed in 4 European cities with measurable outcomes."

### UAE fintech pitch

> "We the UAE 2031's financial-services pillar targets the UAE becoming a top-3 global fintech hub. Our regulatory-tech platform addresses the CBUAE's open-banking framework specifically. We are already engaged with the FSRA RegLab and DFSA Innovation Testing License processes."

### Qatar education pitch

> "Qatar NDS3 targets R&D investment at 1.5% of GDP by 2030, with education and research as priority pillars. Our EdTech platform aligns with Qatar Foundation's Education City strategy and has been piloted with two of the international university partners in Education City."

## Pitch delivery adjustments

### Pacing
- Slower than Western. Pause more. Don't fill silence.
- Expect interruptions for clarification questions. Treat each as engagement, not disruption.
- Allow 50% of the meeting for discussion, 50% for presentation. Western default is the inverse.

### Q&A
- Don't dodge questions. GCC executives expect direct answers, even if "I don't know."
- "Let me come back to you on that within 48 hours" is a strong response if you genuinely don't know.
- Don't say "great question" — reads as Western tech-bro affectation.

### Language
- Speak slightly slower than your natural pace.
- Avoid Americanisms ("home run," "slam dunk," "low-hanging fruit").
- Use precise technical terms; many GCC executives are technically sophisticated.
- Acronyms: spell out the first time, even if you think they're obvious (PDPL, ICV, RHQ).

## What outside founders should know

- **The same deck works across all GCC countries with just the Vision-pillar slide swapped.** Build the deck once with a single replaceable slide, not 6 separate decks.
- **Have an Arabic-translated 2-page version of the deck.** Leave-behinds in Arabic are circulated internally much more than English versions.
- **Don't promise capabilities you don't have just to win the deal.** GCC enterprise customers have long memories; over-promising once kills you for the entire region.
- **Send the deck AFTER the meeting, not before.** Many GCC buyers prefer to discover the product in conversation, not to be primed by the deck. Exception: regulator/sandbox applications often require pre-meeting decks.

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't put a pricing slide as slide 3 or 4. Hold until at least slide 9.
- ❌ Don't show competitor logos in a "we beat X" frame. GCC buyers find this aggressive.
- ❌ Don't use animated GIFs / TikTok-style elements in formal pitches.
- ❌ Don't have a slide that says "We're not VC-backed" thinking it's a virtue signal. Local capital welcomes VC backing.
- ❌ Don't disclose other GCC customer names without permission. Confidentiality norms are stricter than the US.

## Verify before sending the deck

- Vision pillar reference is current and correct for the target country.
- All currency conversions use current pegged rates.
- VAT inclusive vs exclusive is clearly stated.
- All claimed regional customer references have given permission for naming.
- Color palette and imagery is appropriate for the target audience.
- Spelling of recipient name, title, organization is verified.
