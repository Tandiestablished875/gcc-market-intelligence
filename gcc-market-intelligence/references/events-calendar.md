# GCC Events Calendar — what to attend, what to skip, what they cost

> Last verified: May 2026. Dates and pricing change every cycle. Verify before booking flights.

## How to use this file

Events in the GCC are a primary B2B marketing channel — bigger and more concentrated than in most other regions. A 4-day flagship event in Riyadh, Dubai, Doha, or Manama can compress what would otherwise be 3 months of meeting-booking into a single trip.

But events are also a major sink for capital and energy. Booths, sponsorships, dinners, travel, junior team coverage — easily $30k–$200k+ per major event. The right question is always: does this event have my buyers, and can I get the meetings without paying the full sponsorship?

## The fundamental rule

**At every GCC event, book side meetings before you arrive. Don't rely on booth traffic.** The on-floor conversation is signal acquisition. The real deals happen in hotel lobbies, side rooms, dinners, and post-event coffee.

## Saudi Arabia

### LEAP

- **When:** annually; recent editions early autumn — confirm dates on the official site.
- **Where:** Riyadh.
- **Size:** the largest tech event in MENA by attendance — 200k+ visitors reported across 4 days in recent editions.
- **Focus:** tech, AI, cybersecurity, fintech, gaming, healthtech, deeptech.
- **Best for:** any tech founder targeting KSA. The Rocket Fuel pitch competition has had $1M+ prize pools in past editions.
- **Cost realism:** booth costs $10k–$100k+; side-event sponsorships up to $250k. **For most founders, attend without a booth — book ~25 side meetings ahead of time.**
- **Verify:** https://onegiantleap.com.

### FII (Future Investment Initiative)

- **When:** late October annually.
- **Where:** Riyadh (King Abdulaziz International Conference Center).
- **Size:** ~6,000–8,000 invitation-only attendees; senior decision-makers.
- **Focus:** capital allocation, geopolitics, strategic investment.
- **Best for:** founders raising $25M+ rounds with institutional backers; not for early-stage outreach.
- **Cost realism:** invitation by application; sponsorship and VIP packages 6 figures+.
- **Verify:** https://fii-institute.org.

### Biban

- **When:** annually (typically November).
- **Where:** Riyadh.
- **Size:** Monsha'at's flagship SME forum.
- **Focus:** SME finance, entrepreneurship, Saudi market entry.
- **Best for:** SME-stage founders engaging Saudi government SME programs.
- **Verify:** https://www.monshaat.gov.sa.

### Black Hat MEA

- **When:** annually (typically October/November).
- **Where:** Riyadh.
- **Focus:** cybersecurity.
- **Best for:** cybersecurity founders.

## How to evaluate any event

Use this rapid checklist:

1. **Are 5+ of my target buyers attending? Are they at the senior level I need?** If less, skip.
2. **Can I get 10+ pre-booked side meetings before I arrive?** If yes, attend without a booth. If no, skip — the floor traffic alone won't justify the trip.
3. **Is there a co-located government / regulator presence I need?** WGS, FII, GITEX bring federal regulators. Use the access.
4. **What's the cost ratio?** A $30k event with one $500k contract is a clear yes. A $30k event with vague pipeline is a clear no.
5. **Is there pavilion or national-grouping discount?** UK Department for Business & Trade, Germany Trade & Invest, France's Business France, Canada Trade Commissioner Service, Singapore Enterprise SG, Saudi Trade Office, US Commercial Service — all run pavilion programs that subsidize foreign founders into GCC events at 40–80% off retail.

## Calendar overlay

Across all GCC events, the dead months are:
- **Ramadan** (March 2026 — shifts ~11 days earlier each year). Major events generally avoid this window.
- **Hajj** (June–July 2026; varies annually). Saudi commercial life slows.
- **July–August** — Gulf summer. Most senior decision-makers travel to Europe / US. Avoid event-anchored visits.

The peak event windows are:
- **September–November** — every major Riyadh and Dubai event.
- **January–March** — Web Summit Qatar, WGS, IDEX, STEP, Gulfood, Arab Health, F1 Bahrain.

## What outside founders should know

- The fastest path to senior buyers is often **NOT the keynote stage** but the side-event circuit. A founder dinner the night before LEAP or GITEX gets you in front of 12 senior buyers in three hours — at fraction of booth cost.
- **National pavilions are underrated.** UK DBT, France Business France, etc. — they often have unsold slots and will fast-track a foreign founder with a credible product. Free or heavily subsidized booth.
- **Demo days** at Hub71, Sheraa, NTDP, Sanabil 500, NBB Innovation Program (Feb 4 2026) — these are smaller but high-conversion alternatives to mega-events.
- **Don't over-attend.** Pick 2–3 flagship events per year + the right sector show. More than 6 events/year burns your team without commensurate pipeline lift.

## What NOT to do

- Don't pay full-price for a Series A booth unless you've validated your buyer list.
- Don't attend an event purely as a brand exercise unless your CFO has explicitly budgeted it as marketing.
- Don't bring a junior person to a senior-buyer event. Bring the CEO / founder.
- Don't try to convert booth traffic to deals — that's not how Gulf B2B works. Use the booth to schedule the actual meeting.
- Don't show up to a Saudi event in August or to a Doha event during Ramadan unless explicitly invited.

## Verify before booking flights

- Event websites (always). Dates and venues shift quarterly.
- Country-specific event government coordinators for visa fast-tracking (most major events have these).
- Pavilion application deadlines via your home country's trade promotion body.
