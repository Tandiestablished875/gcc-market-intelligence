# Sales Cycles & Calendar — Operating Rhythm in the GCC

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: GCC government holiday calendars, Saudi Aramco / ADNOC fiscal years, regional sales-leader interviews, Islamic calendar references.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

The Gulf business calendar is shaped by **three forces**: the Islamic (Hijri) calendar (Ramadan, Hajj, Eid), the Gregorian fiscal year, and the climate (Jul–Aug is genuinely too hot for productive activity). Founders who ignore this lose 4–6 months of selling time.

- **Sep–early Dec + Jan–mid Mar** = the two main selling windows. ~6 months of real productivity per year.
- **Ramadan** (Feb 17–Mar 18, 2026 / Feb 7–Mar 8, 2027) is NOT dead. Office hours shrink, but iftar / suhoor is the highest-leverage relationship-building window of the year.
- **Hajj** (early–mid Jun 2026, late May 2027) + **summer (Jul–Aug)** = dead. Most decision-makers are out of the country or on extended leave.
- **Two Eids** (Eid al-Fitr immediately after Ramadan; Eid al-Adha during Hajj) are full holiday weeks; decision-making freezes.
- **Weekends differ by country:** KSA + UAE + Bahrain + Qatar + Kuwait + Oman all align **Saturday–Sunday** now (KSA changed from Thu–Fri to Fri–Sat in 2013; UAE made the biggest shift in 2022 from Fri–Sat to Sat–Sun with a half-day Friday). KSA remains **Fri–Sat**.
- **Prayer rhythm** structures the working day: 5 prayers, business pauses around Dhuhr (early afternoon) and Asr (mid-afternoon) — especially in KSA where retail still closes during prayer times.

## The Hijri calendar — what matters

The Islamic year is ~11 days shorter than the Gregorian year. Ramadan, Hajj, and Eid shift earlier each Gregorian year. Key dates change annually.

### Ramadan

**2026:** approximately Feb 17 – Mar 18 (verified by moon-sighting).
**2027:** approximately Feb 7 – Mar 8.
**2028:** approximately Jan 27 – Feb 25.

Working hours:
- KSA: ~6 hours/day for Muslims (Labor Law); UAE: 2 hours less than normal (Federal Decree-Law 33/2021 — applies to all employees in many companies, Muslim and non-Muslim).
- Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Oman: 6-hour working day for Muslims (sectoral variation).
- Government: 9am–2pm or 10am–3pm typical.
- Banking: shortened. Deal-closing happens after iftar (~7–10pm) or in late-night majlis sessions.

**For foreign founders:**
- ❌ Don't fly in for "back-to-back meetings" expecting normal density.
- ✅ Do plan iftar invitations. An iftar invitation is a relationship-building event of substance.
- ✅ Do reach out via email/LinkedIn early in Ramadan — decision-makers are more responsive to thoughtful messages now than at any other time.
- ✅ Do send Ramadan greetings BEFORE Ramadan starts (not during the first week — by then it's stale).

### Eid al-Fitr (end of Ramadan)

3-day official holiday in most GCC countries, often extended to 5 days. Most senior decision-makers are abroad for 7–10 days. **Plan no decisions in this window.**

2026: approximately Mar 19–21 (with extensions).
2027: approximately Mar 9–11.

### Hajj

The annual pilgrimage; 8–12 Dhul Hijjah.

**2026:** approximately Jun 4–9. Day of Arafah (key Hajj day): Jun 8.
**2027:** approximately May 25–30. Day of Arafah: May 29.

KSA: full halt — government workers get a week+. Aramco, government ministries are essentially closed.
UAE/Qatar/Bahrain/Kuwait/Oman: less dramatic but most Muslim decision-makers are unavailable or on Hajj themselves.

### Eid al-Adha

3-day official holiday across all GCC states, immediately following Hajj. Often extended to 4–7 days.

2026: approximately Jun 6–8.
2027: approximately May 27–29.

### Islamic New Year (1 Muharram)

Public holiday in most GCC. ~Jun 25, 2026 / ~Jun 14, 2027. Single day.

### Mawlid al-Nabi (Prophet's Birthday)

Public holiday in most GCC except KSA. ~Aug 25, 2026 / ~Aug 14, 2027.

## The Gregorian calendar — what matters

### Fiscal year and budget cycles

- **KSA government:** fiscal year = Hijri (Muharram to Dhul Hijjah). But increasingly aligned with Gregorian budget cycle. Annual budget announcement in late December.
- **UAE federal:** Gregorian fiscal year (Jan–Dec).
- **PIF, ADNOC, Aramco, sovereign funds:** Gregorian fiscal year (though some still report on Hijri-aligned periods).
- **Most family conglomerates:** Gregorian fiscal year.

**Implication for founders:**
- October–December = budget planning for next year. Pitch decks should reference "FY26 budget allocation" to be relevant.
- January–March = budget release / RFP issuance.
- April–June = implementation and procurement.
- July–August = paused (heat + religious + summer travel).
- September–November = closing the financial year + final-quarter spending push.

### National Day weeks

Major national days double as networking-heavy holiday weeks. Founders should NOT push deals across these but can attend events.

| Country | National Day | Typical window |
|---|---|---|
| Saudi Arabia | September 23 | Sep 22–25 |
| UAE | December 2 (Union Day) | Dec 1–3 |
| Qatar | December 18 | Dec 17–19 |
| Bahrain | December 16 | Dec 15–17 |
| Kuwait | February 25 (National Day) + Feb 26 (Liberation Day) | Feb 24–27 |
| Oman | November 18 | Nov 17–19 |

### Saudi Founding Day

February 22 — declared in 2022, distinct from National Day. Increasingly used for major government announcements.

## The two closing windows

### Window 1: September – early December

- **September** is the "back-to-school" of the GCC. Decisions resume. RFPs issue.
- **Major events cluster here:** Black Hat MEA (KSA), GITEX (UAE), Cybersecurity Forum, ADIPEC (UAE).
- **Mid-December:** UAE National Day → year-end → many decision-makers travel.

### Window 2: January – mid-March

- **January:** new fiscal year budget release. **LEAP** (Riyadh) and **WGS** (Dubai) anchor this window.
- **February:** **FII MENA**, Gulfood, IDEX (biennial). High velocity.
- **March:** Ramadan begins (in 2026 / 2027 / 2028 — early in the year). Window closes.

## The two dead windows

### Dead window 1: Ramadan + Eid al-Fitr

~4–5 weeks of reduced productivity, then 7–10 days of holiday. Plan for ZERO new deal closings in this window — but max relationship-building.

### Dead window 2: Hajj + Summer (June – August)

~10–12 weeks. Government slows after Hajj/Eid al-Adha, then most senior decision-makers travel — London / Geneva / Saint-Tropez / Marbella / the Maldives. Even available staff are typically not authorized to close deals without senior sign-off.

**Founder strategy for the dead windows:**
- Use Ramadan for upstream relationship-building (iftar invitations, warm-intro requests).
- Use summer for content, product, your own team building. Don't burn budget on flights for empty rooms.
- The exception: **August in Dubai is back-to-business by late August.** UAE (especially Dubai) is the first GCC market to "reopen" after summer.

## Weekly rhythm

### Weekend conventions (as of May 2026)

| Country | Weekend |
|---|---|
| Saudi Arabia | Friday–Saturday |
| UAE | Saturday–Sunday (since Jan 2022) |
| Bahrain | Saturday–Sunday |
| Qatar | Friday–Saturday |
| Kuwait | Friday–Saturday |
| Oman | Friday–Saturday |

**Implication:** UAE + Bahrain are aligned with Western markets. KSA + Kuwait + Qatar + Oman are not. **Thursday is the busiest day in KSA, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman** — last working day of the week.

### Daily rhythm

Typical office hours: 8/9am – 5/6pm, with sub-hour breaks for Dhuhr and Asr prayers.

- KSA retail still closes for prayer (10–25 minutes per prayer). Government and corporate offices have prayer rooms; senior meetings rarely interrupted unless explicitly scheduled around prayer.
- UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman: no mandatory closures; prayer rooms available.
- **Avoid scheduling meetings during prayer times** in KSA — Dhuhr (~12:00–12:30pm), Asr (~3:30–4:00pm). Friday prayer (~12:00–1:30pm) is fully blocked.

### Prayer time impact on calendar

Use a prayer-time app (e.g., Muslim Pro, IslamicFinder) to check the exact time for the city + date. Times vary by ~10 minutes month-to-month.

## What outside founders should know

- **Plan trips around the calendar, not against it.** A 10-day Dubai+Riyadh trip in late September is worth 3x the same trip in mid-July.
- **Ramadan is a feature, not a bug** for relationship-building if you adapt. The hardest-to-meet decision-makers will share iftar with you in ways they would never have lunch.
- **Confirm — and re-confirm — meetings.** Meetings shift / get rescheduled more in the GCC than in most Western markets, particularly during transition weeks before/after holidays. A WhatsApp confirmation 24h before is standard practice and not pushy.
- **Senior people travel a lot.** Don't read absence as disinterest. Re-engage in September / January.

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't launch new product in July or August. Nobody will notice.
- ❌ Don't push for contract signature during Ramadan's last 10 days (Laylat al-Qadr; spiritually intensive period).
- ❌ Don't schedule "lunch meetings" during Ramadan (Muslim attendees are fasting). Use breakfast (very early) or iftar.
- ❌ Don't book deals to close in Hajj week. Decision-makers can't sign even if they want to.
- ❌ Don't send promotional marketing emails in the first week of Ramadan — they read as tone-deaf. Send Ramadan greetings instead.
- ❌ Don't use generic "Eid greetings" template emails. Use specific Eid (al-Fitr vs al-Adha) and personalize.

## Verify before committing capital

- **Islamic dates:** moon-sighting committees verify final dates within ~48 hours. Use IslamicFinder or Saudi National Astronomical Calendar for projections.
- **Public holidays:** verify country-specific announcements via official labour ministries (KSA HRSD, UAE MoHRE, Bahrain LMRA, etc.) — extensions are often announced 1–2 weeks before.
- **Working-hour Ramadan rules:** verify with HR counsel in each country — Federal Decree-Law 33/2021 UAE and Labor Law KSA both specify reductions but apply differently to Muslims vs non-Muslims.
