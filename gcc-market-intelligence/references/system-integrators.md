# System Integrators in the GCC — Who You Sell Through, Who You Compete With

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: company press, Mordor Intelligence, AWS / Microsoft partner directories, Etimad tender history. SI partnership terms shift; verify before signing.

## Why this matters

For most foreign B2B / B2G tech founders in the GCC, system integrators (SIs) are both:
1. **The fastest channel to enterprise and government customers** — they hold pre-qualified vendor status, security clearances, RHQ structures, and existing customer relationships.
2. **Your most aggressive competitors** — they offer "build vs buy" alternatives, often with deeper local relationships than yours.

Knowing who buys whose tech, where the channels are, and who has won what against whom is the foundation of GCC enterprise selling.

## Channel-partnership economics — what to expect

When you sign with a GCC SI as a channel partner, expect:

| Term | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Margin / discount | 20–40% | Higher for first deals; tightens as volume grows |
| Exclusivity | Often requested for a country or region | Push back; geographic or sector-specific exclusivity is better than market-wide |
| Term | 2–3 years initial | Renewal clauses important |
| Minimum commitments | Often requested | Get clear written escalation triggers |
| MDF (marketing development funds) | Sometimes provided | Typically 2–5% of resold revenue |
| Pre-sales support | Expected | They expect you on customer calls in-region |
| RHQ requirement (KSA) | Required for any government deal | They have RHQ; you may not need yours immediately if they hold the prime |
| ICV scoring (UAE) | Their certification flows to the deal | A win for you |
| Saudization / Emiratisation pass-through | You may need local FTE if you provide implementation | Plan accordingly |

## Etimad tender history — competitive intelligence

For Saudi government, the public Etimad portal allows filtering tender history by category, awardee, and ministry. This is the most underused competitive-intelligence resource for KSA-targeting founders:

1. Identify a tender category aligned with your product (e.g. "Cybersecurity services," "Cloud infrastructure," "Healthcare IT").
2. Filter by recent awards (24-36 months).
3. Map who has won which workloads in which ministries.
4. Identify the SI most aligned to your category; that's your partnership target.
5. Identify the SI you'll be displacing; that's your competitive target.

TendersAlerts and similar paid services offer AI-aided scraping; for free, Etimad's own search is workable for small categories.

For UAE: **Tamm** (Abu Dhabi government services platform) and **Dubai Now** (Dubai equivalent) publish procurement information, though less comprehensively than Etimad.

## How to choose a channel partner

Quick test:
- Does the SI have a referenceable customer in your sector + government tier within the last 18 months?
- Do they have a pre-sales engineering team that can present your product credibly?
- Do they have an active relationship with the buyer you want to target (verify with a named contact)?
- Do their existing partnerships compete with yours? (e.g. if they already sell IBM cloud, AWS partnership may compete.)
- What's their RHQ / ICV / Saudization score?

## What outside founders should know

- **The SI route is faster than direct selling for first 2–3 deals.** After that, building direct enterprise sales capability becomes higher-margin.
- **Most SIs run a "land grab" strategy on emerging tech.** They will sign your reseller agreement, then explore building competing capability in-house. Plan for this — protect your IP carefully, structure agreements to penalize defection, and watch for customer-facing meetings happening without you.
- **Local FTE matters even when partnered.** A pre-sales engineer based in Riyadh / Dubai / Doha unlocks much faster cycles than the same engineer flying in.

## What NOT to do

- Don't sign exclusive country-wide deals on first agreement. Sector or named-account exclusivity only.
- Don't agree to MDF deductions you can't audit.
- Don't pay co-marketing money upfront. Reimbursement-based MDF only.
- Don't let the SI lead the customer relationship for more than 12 months without a co-presence clause that lets you sit in customer review meetings.
- Don't assume the SI's RHQ / ICV qualifies you. Government buyers may require both sides certified.
