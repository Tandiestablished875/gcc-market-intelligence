# United Arab Emirates (UAE) — Country & Emirates

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: FCSC, SCAD, Dubai Statistics Centre, MoET, Ministry of Economy, Ministry of HR & Emiratisation, official emirate releases. Emiratisation thresholds and ICV scoring change regularly — re-verify before pitching.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

- **Federation of 7 emirates.** Abu Dhabi (capital, oil, sovereign wealth) + Dubai (commerce, logistics, services) + Sharjah (culture, light industry) + Ajman + Umm Al Quwain + Ras Al Khaimah (RAK) + Fujairah. Each emirate has its own ruler, government, courts, and procurement system. Federal law sits above.
- **Population ~10.5M.** Only ~12% are Emirati citizens; ~88% are expats. Per-capita consumer power is high; the consumer market is large despite small national population.
- **2024 federal GDP ~AED 1.76T (~$480B);** non-oil sectors contribute ~75% of federal GDP. Abu Dhabi alone is AED 1.2T in 2024.
- **Two distinct go-to-market motions.** Dubai is fast, marketing-led, deal-oriented. Abu Dhabi is patient, government-led, capital-deployment-oriented. They are NOT the same market.
- **Federal CIT: 9%** above AED 375k profit (since June 2023). Free zones: 0% on qualifying income. **VAT: 5%.** No personal income tax.
- **100% foreign ownership in most mainland sectors** (since 2021 — major liberalization).
- **Emiratisation enforcement is tight in 2026.** Mainland 50+ FTE = 8% by Dec 31, 2025, 10% by 2026. Fine AED 9,000/month per shortfall position. Mainland 20–49 FTE in 14 named sectors: 1 Emirati by Dec 2024 + 1 more by Dec 2025; fine AED 108k Jan 2026 if missed.
- **Free zones (DIFC, ADGM, JAFZA, Hub71, Sharjah Media City, RAKEZ, IFZA, Meydan, DMCC, etc.) are exempt from Emiratisation,** allow 100% foreign ownership, and let you ship pilots and SaaS deals fast. Most foreign founders should free-zone-license first, mainland later.
- **Sovereign wealth concentration in Abu Dhabi.** ADIA (~$1.11T), Mubadala (most active SWF globally in 2025 with $33.7B deployed), ADQ (~$263B), plus IHC (~$240B market cap conglomerate, royal-linked).
- **Best closing windows: October–December, February–April.** Avoid: Ramadan (relationship yes, contracts no), July–August summer, last 2 weeks of December (most expat decision-makers travel).

## Federal vs. emirate authority

- **Federal:** foreign policy, defense, monetary policy (Central Bank UAE / CBUAE), federal taxation (Ministry of Finance, FTA), federal courts, telecom (TDRA), data protection (UAE Federal PDPL since 2022 + emirate-level layers like DIFC DPL and ADGM Data Protection Regulations), aviation (GCAA), nationwide labor / Emiratisation (MoHRE).
- **Emirate-level:** local economic development authority, procurement, real estate, planning, health, education, transport, electricity / water, police. The Department of Economy & Tourism (DET in Dubai), Abu Dhabi Department of Economic Development (ADDED), Sharjah Economic Development Department (SEDD), etc.

## National strategies

- **We the UAE 2031** (Nov 22, 2022): GDP AED 1.49T → 3T; tourism GDP → AED 450B; 40M hotel guests/yr by 2031.
- **UAE Centennial 2071:** long-horizon — best country by 2071.
- **Operation 300bn (Industrial):** triple manufacturing GDP to AED 300B by 2031.
- **UAE National AI Strategy 2031:** first country to appoint a Minister of State for AI (Omar Sultan Al Olama, 2017). Designed to make UAE a global AI hub.
- **UAE Net Zero 2050** — first GCC country to commit; COP28 host (Dec 2023). Year of Sustainability 2024–25.
- **Make it in the Emirates** — annual industrial flagship event; the procurement showcase for Operation 300bn.

## Emirate-by-emirate

---

### Abu Dhabi — the capital, the wealth, the long-game buyer

#### Macro

- **Population:** ~4.14M (2024); growing fast.
- **GDP 2024:** AED 1.2T (~$326B) — by far the largest contributor to UAE GDP (~68%).
- **Non-oil share of Abu Dhabi GDP:** 54.7% in 2024; 56.8% in Q2 2025 (first time non-oil crossed 56% in a Q2). 18 consecutive quarters of growth as of Q3 2025.
- **Oil reserves:** holds ~94% of UAE's oil; ADNOC is the producer.
- **Sectors that grew fastest 2024–25:** transportation & storage (+16.9% in 2024), construction (+11.3% to +13.9% in Q3 2025), finance & insurance (+10.7%), electricity/gas/water (+12.5% to +16.2%).

#### Government

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan ("MBZ") — President of the UAE since May 2022.
- **Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi:** Sheikh Khaled bin Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan (since March 2023).
- **Deputy Ruler & National Security Adviser:** Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan — chairs ADQ, IHC, First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB); since 2023 also chairs ADIA. De facto business chief of the Al Nahyan family. Oversees G42 / Core42 / MGX. See `references/families/uae-ruling-families.md`.
- **Deputy Ruler:** Sheikh Hazza bin Zayed Al Nahyan.
- **Deputy Prime Minister:** Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed Al Nahyan — chairs Mubadala.
- **Executive Council:** Abu Dhabi's cabinet-equivalent.
- **Abu Dhabi Department of Economic Development (ADDED):** the licensing and economic policy body. Chairman: Ahmed Jasim Al Zaabi.
- **Statistics Centre Abu Dhabi (SCAD):** the macro data source.
- **Abu Dhabi Investment Office (ADIO):** the FDI agency.

#### Where the budget is

- ADQ-portfolio companies (e2, Emirates Steel Arkan, Modon, Etihad Rail, Etihad Aviation, Pure Health, etc.).
- IHC subsidiaries (Multiply Group, Alpha Dhabi, Pal Cooling, etc.) — see `references/families/uae-ruling-families.md`.
- ADNOC and its subsidiaries (ADNOC Distribution, ADNOC Logistics, ADNOC Drilling, ADNOC Gas) — major tech buyer.
- G42 / Core42 / Inception Institute of AI / Presight — AI / sovereign cloud / digital health.
- Department of Health (DoH) and its purchasing arm — for healthtech.
- Department of Culture & Tourism (DCT) — for tourism / hospitality / culture tech.
- Department of Municipalities and Transport (DMT) — for smart-city / infrastructure tech.
- Tamm — the unified digital citizen platform; powerful procurement signal.

#### Free zones / soft-landing in Abu Dhabi

- **Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM)** — financial free zone, English common law jurisdiction, fintech-friendly, Independent RegLab regulatory sandbox.
- **Hub71** — Mubadala + Govt of Abu Dhabi tech ecosystem. Up to AED 750k incentives. Verticals: AI, ClimateTech, Digital Assets, Life Sciences, SAVI mobility, ECA Anjal Z. See `references/soft-landing-programs.md`.
- **KEZAD (Khalifa Economic Zones Abu Dhabi)** — industrial / logistics.
- **Masdar City Free Zone** — clean tech and renewables.
- **twofour54** — media / content.

#### What outside founders should know

- Abu Dhabi rewards patience. Sales cycles are 12–18 months for institutional deals. Family-office / SWF-portfolio deals slower.
- Government strategy is highly coordinated — winning one entity often warms you up to others.
- AI is the priority sector for 2024–26: Abu Dhabi Digital Strategy (2025–27) targets the world's first fully AI-native government by 2027. AED 13B budget.
- IHC's market cap (~AED 880B / ~$240B+ late 2025) makes it the dominant ADX-listed entity. Many "private" deals in Abu Dhabi route through IHC subsidiaries.

### Dubai — commerce, services, speed

#### Macro

- **Population:** ~3.8M (2024); fastest-growing major Gulf city.
- **GDP 2024:** ~AED 500B+ (~$136B+); Dubai is roughly the federal #2 after Abu Dhabi.
- **Sector mix:** wholesale & retail trade (~24%), transportation & storage (~12%, including airline + ports), financial services (~12%), real estate (~7–8%), construction, manufacturing, tourism, ICT.
- **Tourism:** 17.15M international visitors in 2023; targeting 25M+ by 2025 and beyond.
- **DXB airport:** world's busiest by international passenger volume (~87M in 2023; ~100M expected by 2027).

#### Government

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum ("MBR") — Vice President & Prime Minister of UAE since 2006.
- **Crown Prince of Dubai:** Sheikh Hamdan bin Mohammed Al Maktoum ("Fazza") — Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Defence (since 2024). The active operational leader of Dubai.
- **Deputy Rulers of Dubai:** Sheikh Maktoum bin Mohammed (Deputy PM, Minister of Finance) and Sheikh Ahmed bin Mohammed.
- **Sheikh Ahmed bin Saeed Al Maktoum** — uncle of the Ruler; runs Emirates Airline, Dubai Airports, ICD (Dubai sovereign fund), Dubai Holding. The single most powerful business executive in Dubai.
- **Dubai Department of Economy & Tourism (DET):** the licensing and tourism/promotion body.
- **Dubai Future Foundation (DFF):** the innovation and emerging-tech engagement body.
- **Smart Dubai (now Digital Dubai):** the digital government office.

#### Where the budget is

- **Dubai Holding** — 99.67% owned by Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid; one of the largest landowners globally (26M sqm+). Subsidiaries: Jumeirah Group (hospitality), TECOM (Dubai Internet City, Dubai Media City, Dubai Knowledge Park — most free-zone tenants live in these), Dubai Properties Group, Emirates International Telecommunications.
- **ICD (Investment Corporation of Dubai)** — the Dubai government sovereign fund (~$300B). Holds Emirates Group, ENBD (Emirates NBD), du, Dubai Aluminium, Emaar Properties stakes.
- **Emaar Properties** — the Burj Khalifa / Dubai Mall / Dubai Creek Harbour developer.
- **Nakheel** — Palm Jumeirah developer; part of Dubai World.
- **Dubai World** — DP World (global logistics / ports), Drydocks, Nakheel.
- **RTA (Roads & Transport Authority)** — Dubai Metro, taxis, autonomous mobility pilots.
- **DEWA (Dubai Electricity & Water Authority)** — utilities + solar park (the world's largest single-site solar park).
- **DHA (Dubai Health Authority)** — public healthcare procurement.
- **Dubai Police** — major tech buyer (smart policing, AI surveillance).

#### Dubai-specific strategies

- **Dubai Economic Agenda D33** (Jan 4, 2023): AED 32T (~$8.7T) cumulative economic activity by 2033; FDI AED 32B/yr → AED 60B/yr (AED 650B cumulative); 30 future-sector unicorns; 100 transformational projects.
- **Dubai 2040 Urban Master Plan** — urban-planning vision.
- **Dubai AI Festival, Dubai FinTech Summit** — Apr/May annual events.

#### Free zones / soft-landing in Dubai

- **DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre)** — financial free zone, English common law; DIFC Innovation Hub + FinTech Hive accelerator.
- **DMCC (Dubai Multi Commodities Centre)** — commodities + crypto; "Crypto Centre" houses 600+ Web3 firms; #1 free zone globally for years.
- **TECOM zones** — Dubai Internet City (DIC), Dubai Media City (DMC), Dubai Knowledge Park, Dubai Studio City, Dubai Production City, Dubai Industrial City. **in5** is the TECOM startup-incubation arm.
- **JAFZA (Jebel Ali Free Zone)** — industrial, logistics; near Jebel Ali Port (one of world's busiest container ports).
- **DAFZA (Dubai Airport Free Zone)** — adjacent to DXB.
- **IFZA, Meydan Free Zone, Dubai South** — newer, cheaper licensing options.
- **Dubai Future Accelerators / Area 2071** — DFF's startup pipeline.

#### What outside founders should know

- Dubai is the easiest GCC entry point for foreign founders. Faster company setup, broader English usage, more expats in decision-making roles, looser cultural protocol.
- Dubai is NOT a buying center for Saudi-government work. If your target is KSA government, route through Riyadh, not Dubai.
- Many founders use Dubai as a regional HQ for the GCC. Tax-efficient (free-zone CIT 0% on qualifying income; no withholding); broad ecosystem; visa friendly (Golden Visa, Green Visa for professionals).
- Marketing-led GTM works in Dubai (paid acquisition, content, events). Less so in Abu Dhabi.

### Sharjah — the cultural emirate, light industry, family-oriented commerce

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Dr. Sultan bin Muhammad Al Qasimi (Al Qasimi dynasty, branch ruling Sharjah; in power since 1972). Academic — University of Exeter, Durham. Author of *The Myth of Arab Piracy in the Gulf*.
- **Crown Prince & Deputy Ruler:** Sheikh Sultan bin Mohammed bin Sultan Al Qasimi (since 1999).
- **Population:** ~1.8M.
- **Economy:** more diversified than Abu Dhabi/Dubai in proportion — manufacturing, light industry, education (American University of Sharjah, University of Sharjah), publishing/printing, religious tourism. Lower oil dependence.
- **Notable institutions:**
  - **Sheraa (Sharjah Entrepreneurship Centre)** — 450+ startups; 51% women-led ventures; Access Sharjah Challenge ($136k prizes).
  - **Sharjah Research, Technology & Innovation Park (SRTIP)** — research-led tech zone.
  - **SAIF Zone, Hamriyah Free Zone, Sharjah Publishing City Free Zone, Sharjah Media City Free Zone (Shams)** — multiple free zones, low cost.
- **What outside founders should know:** Sharjah is conservative culturally (the only emirate that bans alcohol). Cheaper licensing than Dubai. Good for media, publishing, edtech, manufacturing. Sheraa is one of the most founder-friendly programs in the region.

---

### Ras Al Khaimah (RAK) — northernmost, industrial, tourism-pivoting

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Saud bin Saqr Al Qasimi (Al Qasimi dynasty, separate branch from Sharjah; in power since 2010). University of Michigan (Economics & Political Science).
- **Crown Prince:** Sheikh Mohammed bin Saud bin Saqr Al Qasimi (b. 1987).
- **Population:** ~500k+.
- **Economy:** RAK Ceramics (one of world's largest ceramics manufacturers), Gulf Pharmaceutical Industries (Julphar — major regional pharma), cement, tourism (Jebel Jais — UAE's highest peak; Wynn Resort under construction on Marjan Island — to open 2027 as first integrated resort/casino in MENA region).
- **Notable institutions:**
  - **RAKEZ (Ras Al Khaimah Economic Zone)** — competitive licensing from AED 7,500.
  - **Sheikh Saud bin Saqr Al Qasimi Foundation** — social/cultural/economic development.
- **What outside founders should know:** RAK is the cheapest GCC mainland-quality jurisdiction. Strong manufacturing base. Tourism opening up rapidly (Wynn Resort changes the gaming/leisure landscape for the entire region). Less competitive for talent than Dubai but more affordable to set up.

### Ajman — smallest, manufacturing-focused

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Humaid bin Rashid Al Nuaimi (Al Nuaimi dynasty; in power since 1981).
- **Crown Prince:** Sheikh Ammar bin Humaid Al Nuaimi.
- **Population:** ~570k.
- **Economy:** trade, manufacturing, real estate, port. Ajman Free Zone (AFZ) is the main entry vehicle.
- **What outside founders should know:** Niche. Mostly relevant if cost-minimization is the priority. Otherwise route through Sharjah, RAK, or Dubai.

---

### Umm Al Quwain (UAQ) — fishing, tourism, low-density

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Saud bin Rashid Al Mualla (Al Mualla dynasty, descendants of the Al Ali tribe; in power since 2009).
- **Crown Prince:** Sheikh Rashid bin Saud bin Rashid Al Mualla.
- **Population:** ~80k — smallest by population.
- **Economy:** fishing, traditional commerce, tourism, some manufacturing. UAQ Free Trade Zone.
- **What outside founders should know:** rarely relevant unless you're in fishing, aquaculture, or seeking the cheapest possible UAE residency option.

---

### Fujairah — east coast, strategic for shipping (Strait of Hormuz alternative)

- **Ruler:** Sheikh Hamad bin Mohammed Al Sharqi (Al Sharqi dynasty, originating from the Sharqiyin tribe; in power since 1974). Educated UK (Mons Officer Cadet School).
- **Crown Prince:** Sheikh Mohammed bin Hamad Al Sharqi.
- **Population:** ~290k.
- **Economy:** strategic position — Port of Fujairah is the world's second-largest bunkering hub (after Singapore). Fujairah is on the east coast, outside the Strait of Hormuz — important for energy security and oil-export contingencies. Refineries, oil storage, mining.
- **Notable institutions:**
  - **Fujairah Free Zone** (one of the oldest in UAE).
  - **Fujairah Oil Industry Zone (FOIZ).**
- **What outside founders should know:** Niche but strategic. If your product touches maritime, energy logistics, or Strait-of-Hormuz contingency planning, Fujairah matters disproportionately.

## What to do — playbook for an outside founder targeting UAE

**Quarter 1 — license and orient**
- Choose mainland vs free-zone. Free-zone first is the default for most foreign founders.
- For tech / SaaS: DIFC, ADGM, DMCC, in5, Hub71, Sheraa, RAKEZ. For industrial: KEZAD, JAFZA, Hamriyah. For media / content: twofour54, Sharjah Media City.
- Apply for residence visa (Golden Visa if you qualify — 10-year visa for investors, entrepreneurs, specialized talent, ≥AED 2M property investment, or ≥AED 2M business investment).
- Open corporate bank account (FAB, ENBD, ADCB, Mashreq, RAKBank — RAKBank and Mashreq friendliest for startups).
- Decide on Emiratisation strategy if you plan to scale past 50 mainland FTE.

**Quarter 2 — soft-land and find your first reference**
- Apply to Hub71 (AI / climate / Web3 / life sciences / mobility), in5, Sheraa, DFF accelerator, ADGM RegLab if regulated.
- Attend GITEX Global / Expand North Star (Dec 7–11, 2026) — book side meetings, don't just exhibit.
- Attend WGS (World Government Summit — Feb annually) if you sell to government.
- Run pilot with one of: ADQ portfolio company, IHC subsidiary, ADNOC, ADIO, DP World, RTA Dubai, DHA, DoH Abu Dhabi.

**Quarter 3 — convert and recruit**
- Recruit local commercial / partnerships lead.
- Set up Emiratisation pipeline if approaching thresholds — government provides Nafis subsidies for Emirati hiring.
- Begin pitching family conglomerates if relevant — see `references/families/uae-merchant-families.md` for who buys what.

**Quarter 4 — scale**
- Consider opening a separate Abu Dhabi entity if Abu Dhabi is the target market — different free zone, different procurement protocols.
- Attend Abu Dhabi Finance Week (Dec).

## What NOT to do

- Don't assume "UAE" = "Dubai". They're two different commercial cultures.
- Don't ignore Emiratisation thresholds — they bite quickly and the fines compound.
- Don't pitch a non-Emirati owner during the discovery call. Emirati ownership is rare in startups but valued in incumbents; you don't have to be Emirati-owned to win, but knowing the local nationality politics matters.
- Don't drink at a business meeting unless your host has clearly opened the door.
- Don't be late. Punctuality matters in Abu Dhabi government even more than in Dubai private.

## Verify before committing capital

- Emiratisation status: MoHRE (https://www.mohre.gov.ae) — quotas tightened multiple times 2023–2026.
- ICV scoring: ADNOC / MoIAT publish ICV certification methodology — different from "Saudi Made"; UAE-mainland-versus-free-zone scoring differs.
- Free-zone CIT 0% qualifying income: FTA guidance + Cabinet Decision 100/2023; "qualifying activities" list has been amended.
- Tamm and Etihad procurement: Tamm.abudhabi for digital services; ADNOC procurement portal for energy supply chain.
- PDPL (federal): Cabinet Decision 9/2023 + UAE Data Office guidance.
