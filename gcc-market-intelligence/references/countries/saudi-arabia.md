# Saudi Arabia (KSA) — Country & Cities

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: GASTAT, MISA, SAMA, World Bank, IMF, official ministry releases. RHQ counts, Saudization tiers, and PIF portfolio status change frequently — re-verify before pitching at scale.

## TL;DR — What the founder needs to know in 60 seconds

- **Population ~37.6M** (May 2026 estimate; GASTAT census 2024 = 35.3M). **~55% Saudi national, ~45% expat.** 75% under 65; median age low 30s. Aggregate consumer market is large but split between citizen purchasing power (high) and expat workforce (lower per-capita but very large in number — ~16M expats).
- **2025 GDP ≈ SAR 4.9 trillion (~$1.3T)**, ~$32k–$33k per capita nominal. **Non-oil GDP is now ~53% of total** post-2024 GASTAT rebase. Q1 2026 GDP growth: 2.8% YoY.
- **Vision 2030 is the operating system.** Every pitch, RFP response, and deck should reference it. Sectoral sub-strategies (Tourism, Industrial, Digital, Health) are where the actual budget lives.
- **PIF holds ~$1.15T AUM** (SWFI #5 globally) and is the single largest customer in the kingdom by spend. Domestic deployment 2021–25: ~$199B. Most "giga-projects" are PIF subsidiaries.
- **RHQ Program (Jan 1, 2024) is binding.** Without a Regional HQ license, you cannot bid on most KSA government tenders. ~700+ MNCs licensed by end-2025.
- **Saudization (Nitaqat) tightening through 2028.** Tech, marketing, sales, engineering, accounting all have new quotas.
- **PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law) fully enforceable Sep 14, 2024.** Extraterritorial. SAR 5M max fine; SAR 3M + 2 years prison for unauthorized sensitive-data disclosure. Cross-border transfers regulated.
- **Foreign ownership: 100% in most sectors** since the February 2025 Investment Law replaced the SAGIA/MISA license regime with the Investment Registration Certificate (IRC). Days, not months.
- **Best deal-closing windows: September–November and January–March.** Avoid: Ramadan (relationship-building yes, contract-signing no), Hajj season, July–August summer.

## Government structure relevant to a founder

- **Head of state:** King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (since Jan 2015).
- **Crown Prince & Prime Minister:** Mohammed bin Salman ("MBS"), chairs Council of Economic and Development Affairs (CEDA), chairs PIF. De facto decision-maker on everything.
- **Council of Ministers** chaired by the PM. Ministries usually held by commoners with junior Al Saud as deputies. Key ones for founders: Investment (MISA — Khalid Al-Falih), Communications & IT (MCIT), Industry & Mineral Resources, Health, Tourism, Sport, Hajj & Umrah, Energy (Prince Abdulaziz bin Salman), Foreign Affairs (Prince Faisal bin Farhan).
- **Regional governorates:** 13. All held by senior Al Saud. The Riyadh, Makkah, and Eastern Province governors are the heaviest commercial gatekeepers.
- **Regulators most relevant to B2B/B2G founders:**
  - **MISA** — investment licensing, RHQ.
  - **SAMA** (Saudi Central Bank) — banking, payments, fintech sandbox.
  - **CMA** (Capital Market Authority) — securities, fintech-lab.
  - **CITC** (Communications, Space & Tech Commission, now CST) — telecom, ICT, cloud.
  - **SDAIA** (Saudi Data & AI Authority) — AI, data protection (PDPL regulator; may transition to NDMO).
  - **NCA** (National Cybersecurity Authority) — anything touching critical national infrastructure.
  - **FSA** (Food and Drug Authority) — food, pharma, medical devices, cosmetics.
  - **ZATCA** — tax, customs, VAT, e-invoicing.
  - **Monsha'at** — SME General Authority; quota-setting policy + SME finance programs.
  - **NTDP** (National Technology Development Program) — under MCIT; the deep-tech / tech-talent subsidy machine.

## National strategy: Vision 2030 — the actual content

Launched April 25, 2016. Three pillars: **Vibrant Society, Thriving Economy, Ambitious Nation.** What this translates to in practice for a B2B/B2G founder:

- **Localize content and labor.** Saudization quotas, ICV (in-country value) preferences in tenders, mandatory RHQ to bid for government, Nitaqat tier protection.
- **Diversify away from oil.** Non-oil sectors are where the new RFPs are: tourism, entertainment, sport, logistics, mining, manufacturing, ICT.
- **Mega-projects (giga-projects) as flagship demand drivers.** NEOM, Red Sea, Qiddiya, Diriyah, AlUla, ROSHN, King Salman Park, Mukaab/New Murabba — all PIF subsidiaries. As of January 2026, NEOM is under proposed restructuring (Oxagon → Aramco, Trojena → Ministry of Sport/Qiddiya, Sindalah → Red Sea Global) — NOT FINALIZED. Verify before designing pitches around specific NEOM sub-projects.

**Sectoral sub-strategies you should namecheck depending on your vertical:**
- National Transformation Program (NTP)
- National Industrial Strategy (NIS)
- National Digital Strategy
- National Tourism Strategy (target 150M visitors by 2030)
- Health Sector Transformation Program
- Financial Sector Development Program
- Human Capability Development Program
- Public Investment Fund Program

## Regions and cities

### Riyadh — the political and commercial capital

- **Population:** ~7.95M (May 2026); fastest-growing GCC city.
- **GDP contribution:** by far the largest single contributor to KSA GDP. ~50% of all national private-sector employment.
- **Sales transaction volume:** SAR 225B in 2024 alone (vs SAR 93.8B Jeddah, SAR 32.9B Dammam) — Riyadh is roughly 2.4x Jeddah and 6.8x Dammam in retail transaction value.
- **What's based here:**
  - Royal Court (Diwan Al-Maliki) and Council of Ministers.
  - PIF headquarters (King Abdullah Financial District / KAFD).
  - All major ministry HQs.
  - Aramco's downstream HQ moved to Riyadh (upstream stays in Dhahran).
  - Big-four banks: SNB, Al Rajhi Bank, Riyad Bank, Banque Saudi Fransi.
  - Telcos: stc HQ, Mobily HQ.
  - Most RHQs of MNCs (~700+ as of end-2025, target was 500 by 2030 — overshot 4 years early).
  - Cloud regions: AWS infra region launching 2026, Oracle, Microsoft Azure operational, Google Cloud announced.
- **Mega-projects in/around Riyadh:** King Salman Park (largest urban park globally), Diriyah Gate (heritage tourism), Mukaab / New Murabba (the 400m cube), Qiddiya entertainment city (Six Flags, etc.), Riyadh Metro (operational since 2024), King Salman International Airport (in build).
- **Founder relevance:** if you sell to government or any PIF subsidiary, your customers are physically here. Plan to be in Riyadh in person at least monthly during the sales cycle.

### Jeddah — Red Sea commercial hub, gateway to Mecca

- **Population:** ~5.7M (May 2026); historic merchant city.
- **Economic role:** the historical commercial capital; many old-money merchant families are Jeddah-based (Bin Laden / SBG, Olayan, Juffali, Tamer, Nahdi).
- **Sectors:** trade, logistics (King Abdulaziz Port — major regional port), hospitality (gateway to Hajj/Umrah), tourism (Red Sea Project headquarters nearby), Aramco downstream operations.
- **Mega-projects:** Jeddah Central Project ($20B waterfront redevelopment); proximity to Red Sea Global (the Red Sea + Amaala tourism mega-projects ~3 hours north).
- **Founder relevance:** if you sell to family conglomerates or hospitality / tourism / logistics buyers, Jeddah is the second meeting after Riyadh. Different cultural register — more relaxed than Riyadh, but family-business sales cycles can be longer.

### Dammam / Eastern Province (Sharqiya) — oil capital

- **Population:** Dammam ~1.57M; metropolitan Dammam–Dhahran–Khobar ("DDK") ~4M+.
- **Economic role:** Aramco's upstream operating HQ is in Dhahran. SABIC's industrial heartland is here. Jubail Industrial City and Yanbu (mirror on the Red Sea) are the kingdom's petrochemical engines.
- **Sectors:** oil & gas (Aramco), petrochemicals (SABIC), manufacturing, logistics (King Abdulaziz Port Dammam — King Fahd Industrial Port), heavy industry.
- **Mega-projects:** **King Salman International Maritime Industry & Services Complex** in Ras Al Khair (largest shipyard in the region); SPARK (Saudi Petroleum & Aerospace Research Kingdom — special economic zone).
- **Founder relevance:** if you sell to energy / petrochemicals / heavy industry / maritime, Eastern Province is the buyer base. Aramco Wa'ed Ventures and Aramco Digital are key startup-engagement vehicles. Aramco's procurement is run via "Aramco Industrial Services Co." and "iktva" (in-kingdom total value add) — your ICV percentage matters more here than anywhere else.

### Madinah (Al-Madinah Al-Munawwarah) — second-holiest city, emerging smart-city hub

- **Population:** ~1.85M.
- **Economic role:** historically a religious tourism city; rapidly being repositioned as a smart, sustainable urban innovation hub.
- **Mega-projects:** Pilgrim Experience Program (target 30M pilgrims/year by 2030); urban-mobility transformation; ROSHN's Sedra mega-community.
- **Founder relevance:** **Madinah Tech Cultivator** (madinahcultivator.com) is the dedicated soft-landing program for smart cities / sustainability / mobility / energy / food security startups. Powered by Gingo. Madinah Municipality is an active pilot buyer; the regional government is hungry for foreign tech. Smaller scale than Riyadh but more accessible and more aligned to founder-led pilots. Strong fit for the Madinah Tech Cultivator Cohort 2 founder profile.

### Mecca (Makkah Al-Mukarramah) — religious tourism engine

- **Population:** ~2.52M permanent + millions in transient pilgrims.
- **Sectors:** religious tourism, hospitality, retail, logistics, vertical-mobility (Haram-area pedestrian engineering).
- **Founder relevance:** specialized — relevant if your product touches Hajj / Umrah logistics, crowd management, religious tourism services, food safety, or pilgrim health. Pilgrim Experience Program is a real budget line; Ministry of Hajj & Umrah procures.

### NEOM (under restructuring) — northwest experimental zone

- **Location:** Tabuk region, ~26,500 km² in the northwest, touching Egypt and Jordan via the Red Sea.
- **Status (Jan 2026):** under proposed restructuring per AGBI reporting; original NEOM Company likely to be partially dismantled with sub-projects redistributed:
  - **The Line** — heavily scaled back from original 170km plan to a shorter initial phase.
  - **Trojena** — mountain tourism; lost the 2029 Asian Winter Games hosting per Jan 2026 reports; rumored to move to Ministry of Sport / Qiddiya.
  - **Oxagon** — industrial port city; rumored to move to Aramco.
  - **Sindalah** — luxury island; rumored to move to Red Sea Global.
  - **NEOM Green Hydrogen Company** — joint venture with Air Products and ACWA Power; continues independently.
- **Budget reality:** WSJ-leaked board memo (Mar 2025) projected $8.8T to 2080; PIF cut up to 60% of budgets across 100+ portfolio companies at Dec 2024 review.
- **Founder relevance:** still a major buyer of foreign tech in deep-tech, energy, mobility, AI, biotech, materials. NEOM Investment Office is the front door. Be aware that organizational structure is in flux — get current contacts via MISA or PIF before designing a NEOM-specific pitch.

### Eastern Province additional cities

- **Khobar** — services hub adjacent to Dhahran/Aramco.
- **Jubail** — industrial city; petrochemicals manufacturing cluster.
- **Hofuf / Al-Ahsa** — agricultural / date-palm region.
- **Ras Al Khair** — shipbuilding and mineral processing SEZ.

### Special Economic Zones (post-2023 SEZ Law)

- **King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC) SEZ** — logistics, light industry; near Jeddah / Red Sea.
- **Jazan SEZ** — agriculture, fishing, processing; southwest.
- **Ras Al Khair SEZ** — maritime, heavy industry; Eastern Province.
- **Cloud Computing SEZ** — virtual zone; relevant for SaaS / data-residency licensing.
- **SILZ (Special Integrated Logistics Zone)** — near King Khalid International Airport, Riyadh.

## What to do — playbook for an outside founder targeting KSA

**Quarter 1 — orient and license**
- Apply for an Investment Registration Certificate (IRC) via MISA — replaces the old SAGIA license; days not months for most sectors. Minimum capital varies but ~SAR 500k common; SAR 30M+ for fully foreign trading/retail.
- Decide on RHQ structure if you plan to bid for government tenders. RHQ requires ≥15 FTE in year one, ≥3 C-suite, mandatory activities within 6 months, quarterly board meetings physically in KSA. Reward: 0% CIT + 0% withholding for 30 years on qualifying activities; 10-year Saudization exemption; unlimited visas.
- Open a corporate bank account (SNB, Al Rajhi, Riyad Bank, Banque Saudi Fransi most common for foreign-founded entities).
- Engage a Saudi-qualified lawyer (Hadef, Clyde & Co, Al Tamimi, Baker McKenzie all have strong KSA presences).
- Begin Etimad supplier registration if pursuing government tenders.

**Quarter 2 — soft-land and find your first reference**
- Apply to Garage by KAUST/STC/MCIT (technology accelerator), Misk Innovation, NTDP Tech Crew (50% salary subsidy up to 18 months), Sanabil 500 Global ($100k SAFE).
- Sector-specific: SAMA Fintech Sandbox, CMA Fintech Lab, SDAIA, Aramco Wa'ed.
- For deep-tech / smart-cities / mobility / energy / food security: apply to **Madinah Tech Cultivator** — the dedicated pilot pathway for the sectors most aligned with municipal smart-city procurement.
- Attend **LEAP** (Aug 31 – Sep 3, 2026 in Riyadh) — book side meetings, not just booth time. Rocket Fuel pitch competition $1M prize pool.
- Attend **Biban** (November, Monsha'at SME forum) for SME relationships.

**Quarter 3 — convert pilot to paid contract**
- Run paid pilot (default: 3–6 months, 50–200k SAR depending on scope). Negotiate the right to use the customer's logo as reference.
- Recruit a Saudi commercial lead — without one, year-two scaling is brittle.
- Begin Saudization plan if you will cross thresholds (most foreign-founded ventures cross them at ~5 FTE).

**Quarter 4 — scale**
- Attend **FII** (late October at KAICC Riyadh) if you have institutional backing.
- Run Etimad tender history analysis for who is winning your category — see `references/system-integrators.md`.
- File RHQ application if you're going to bid government work at scale.

## What NOT to do

- Don't show up in August. The country is closed for business in any meaningful sense.
- Don't sign a "Saudi agent / sponsor" agreement with the first person who offers — agents earn 5–10% on resulting business and contracts are hard to exit.
- Don't pitch NEOM-specific work without first verifying current organizational structure (it's restructuring as of Jan 2026).
- Don't underestimate the importance of in-person presence. Zoom-only sales cycles to KSA private and government buyers fail more often than they succeed.
- Don't quote prices in any currency other than SAR (or USD with SAR cross-rate explicitly noted). VAT-inclusive vs exclusive must be specified.
- Don't bring up Israel, Yemen, Iran, or domestic politics unless the counterparty leads. Stick to commercial topics.

- PIF portfolio status: PIF press releases (https://www.pif.gov.sa); NEOM restructuring not finalized as of Jan 2026.
