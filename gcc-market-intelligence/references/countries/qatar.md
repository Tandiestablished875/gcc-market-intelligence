# Qatar — Country & Doha

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Planning & Statistics Authority (PSA), QCB, Invest Qatar, Qatar Government Communications Office.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

- **Population ~3M.** ~12% Qatari national, ~88% expat. Per-capita GDP is the **highest in the world** by some measures (~$80k+ nominal).
- **GDP 2024 ~$220B.** Hydrocarbon-heavy — LNG dominates. Qatar Energy is the single largest economic actor; the LNG expansion to **142 mtpa by 2030** (~85% increase from current) is the largest single capex program in the country.
- **National Vision 2030** + **Third National Development Strategy (NDS3) 2024–30.** NDS3 targets: 4% GDP CAGR, R&D 1.5% of GDP, **FDI target $100B**, 6M visitors / QAR 34B tourism spend by 2030, 100% electric public bus fleet by 2030.
- **Corporate tax: 10% on foreign-sourced profits.** No VAT (yet — repeatedly postponed). No personal income tax. **QFC (Qatar Financial Centre)** offers a separate 10% CIT regime.
- **One major city = Doha.** ~80%+ of population lives in Doha metro. Lusail (newly built city north of Doha) is the major satellite.
- **Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) ~$475–500B AUM.** Less hostile than KSA, more selective than UAE — QIA invests in mature companies and external funds, not directly in early-stage Western tech.
- **Web Summit Qatar** is the central event — annually January/February, ~25,000 attendees, the largest startup event in MENA. Next: Jan 31 – Feb 3, 2027.
- **Qatari business is concentrated.** ~10 family-owned conglomerates handle ~90% of private-sector commerce. Building relationships with one or two is the door to most others.

## Key institutions for B2B/B2G founders

- **QIA (Qatar Investment Authority)** — sovereign wealth fund, ~$475–500B.
- **Qatar Energy (formerly Qatar Petroleum)** — state-owned hydrocarbon supermajor; CEO Saad Al-Kaabi.
- **Qatar Free Zones Authority (QFZ)** — manages Ras Bufontas (near Hamad International Airport) and Umm Alhoul (deepwater port) free zones.
- **Qatar Financial Centre (QFC)** — onshore financial free zone, separate regulator (QFCRA), English common law.
- **Qatar Science & Technology Park (QSTP)** — research-led free zone in Doha; under Qatar Foundation.
- **Qatar Development Bank (QDB)** — runs Startup Qatar Investment Program: up to **$500k early-stage; up to $5M growth-stage** for foreign startups soft-landing.
- **Qatar Foundation (QF)** — education, research, social development; runs Education City.
- **Invest Qatar (IPA Qatar)** — investment promotion agency. Runs Startup Qatar Pavilion at Web Summit Qatar (1,634 companies registered, 156 licenses issued at the Feb 2025 edition).

## Doha — the city

- **Population:** ~2.4M+ metro (~80% of country).
- **Districts of commercial relevance:**
  - **West Bay** — financial district, most MNC offices, QFC, QFCRA.
  - **Msheireb Downtown Doha** — smart-city redevelopment; the world's first fully sustainable downtown regeneration.
  - **The Pearl** — luxury residential and retail island.
  - **Education City** — universities (Carnegie Mellon, Georgetown, Northwestern, Cornell, Texas A&M, HEC Paris, UCL), QSTP, Sidra Medicine.
  - **Lusail** — newly built city ~20km north; home to Lusail Stadium (2022 World Cup final venue); Lusail Marina; major residential expansion.
  - **Hamad International Airport (DOH)** — consistently ranked top global airport.
  - **Hamad Port** — deepwater port, the logistics anchor.

## National strategy: NDS3 (2024–30)

- 4% real GDP CAGR; non-hydrocarbon CAGR 4%.
- R&D 1.5% of GDP.
- FDI target $100B.
- Tourism: 6M visitors/yr; QAR 34B tourism spend by 2030.
- LNG: 142 mtpa.
- 100% electric public bus fleet by 2030.
- Industrial: cement, petrochemicals, aluminium, manufacturing growth.
- Digital economy targets: digital GDP 3% by 2027, 6% by 2030.

## Where foreign founders typically struggle

- **Hyper-concentrated buying.** The market is small in absolute terms; once you've pitched the top 10 family groups and the relevant ministries, you've pitched ~80% of the addressable market.
- **Geopolitical sensitivity.** Qatar has its own foreign policy line (e.g. Muslim Brotherhood relations, Hamas mediation, Iran ties) — pitches that lean heavily on regional ally framing should know the room.
- **Relationships > everything.** Even more concentrated than Saudi or UAE — one wrong pitch can echo across the entire market.

## What outside founders should know

- The single most efficient first move is to apply to **Startup Qatar Investment Program** (QDB) — up to $500k early-stage, up to $5M growth-stage, and a **fast-track corporate license** at Web Summit Qatar each year.
- **Web Summit Qatar (Jan/Feb annually)** is the highest-leverage event. The Startup Qatar Pavilion offered same-day business registration in 2025 — this changes the market-entry economics dramatically.
- **QFC license** is the cleanest legal structure for foreign service-sector founders — onshore, English common law, 10% CIT only on local-source profits, 100% foreign ownership, free profit repatriation, no FX controls.
- **QSTP** is best for deep-tech R&D collaboration (Qatar Foundation grant access, university partnerships).
- Qatar is rarely "first" GCC entry — most founders pick KSA or UAE first, then add Qatar. Exception: if your product is in LNG, sports tech, education research, or sovereign cloud, Qatar may be priority.
