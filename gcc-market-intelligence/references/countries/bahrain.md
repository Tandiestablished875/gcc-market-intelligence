# Bahrain — Country & Manama

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: iGA (Information & eGovernment Authority), CBB, Bahrain EDB, official ministry releases.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

- **Population ~1.6M.** ~45% Bahraini, ~55% expat. Small absolute market.
- **GDP 2024 ~$45B.** Most diversified GCC economy — non-oil GDP **>80% of total** (best in the region by share).
- **Economic Vision 2030** + forthcoming **Vision 2050.** Pillars: sustainability, fairness, competitiveness.
- **Fastest-track market entry in the GCC.** EDB Fast-Track: free concierge service for foreign investors (residency, business registration, grants, customer intros). 3–5 day Commercial Registration (CR) setup.
- **Operational cost advantage:** EDB cites 30–41% lower than regional peers depending on sector (manufacturing 41% per 2024 EDB annual report; financial services 27% per KPMG 2022). Talent costs ~30% lower than Saudi/UAE for equivalent skills.
- **Financial-services hub legacy.** Hosts the most banking licenses per capita in the region. CBB Regulatory Sandbox (since June 2017) — up to 12 months, BHD 100 fee, 15-day review, open to foreign + joint applications.
- **Corporate tax: 0%** for most sectors (oil & gas 46%). VAT: 10% (raised from 5% in Jan 2022). No personal income tax.
- **Tamkeen (Labour Fund)** — major SME grant engine; BHD 830M cumulative private-sector injection; supported 41,420 Bahrainis + 8,700 enterprises in 2024 alone.
- **Foreign-founder gotcha:** most Tamkeen grants require Bahraini employee on payroll. Solve by incorporating a Bahrain CR + hiring ≥1 Bahraini.

## Key institutions for B2B/B2G founders

- **Bahrain EDB (Economic Development Board):** the FDI agency. Chair: Crown Prince Sheikh Salman bin Hamad. The Fast-Track concierge service is here.
- **Tamkeen (Labour Fund)** — established 2006; major SME / employment support engine. Programs:
  - **Business Development Program** — 50% co-match support.
  - **5K Support** — BHD 5,000 grant cap.
  - **Start Your Business** — incubation.
  - **National Employment Program 3.0** — wage subsidies for hiring Bahrainis.
  - **Raise Program** (Spring Venture Services + Salica) — direct startup funding.
  - **Overseas Expansion** — supports Bahrain companies expanding to KSA / GCC.
- **CBB (Central Bank of Bahrain)** — runs the **Regulatory Sandbox** (since June 2017): up to 12 months operation, BHD 100 fee, 15-day review, rolling intake, open to foreign + joint applications. Approves fintech, crypto, payments, RegTech, InsurTech.
- **FinHub 973** — CBB + Fintech Galaxy joint platform.
- **Bahrain FinTech Bay (BFB)** — 10,000 sqft fintech hub in Manama.
- **NBB Innovation Program** — National Bank of Bahrain accelerator; demo day Feb 4, 2026 ($10k + 3-month incubation + NBB pilot).
- **Mumtalakat** — sovereign wealth fund ~$18B; runs **Bahrain Mumtalakat Venture Capital (BMVC)**.
- **BDB (Bahrain Development Bank)** — runs **Private Credit SME Growth Fund $265M** (launched 2024); BDB Tech Fund $100M.
- **iGA (Information & eGovernment Authority)** — government IT procurement.

## National strategy

### Economic Vision 2030 (current)

Three pillars: Sustainability, Fairness, Competitiveness. Targets: doubling household disposable income (real terms), high-quality jobs, diversification beyond oil.

### Vision 2050 (announced but not formally launched as of latest sources)

Successor framework; verify launch status before referencing in pitches.

### Bahrain Economic Recovery Plan (2021–25)

Capex commitment ~$30B+ in strategic projects. Includes:
- 5 strategic projects: Manama Eastside (waterfront), Bilaj Al Jazayer beach, Bahrain Metro, Fasht Al Adhom (housing), Bahrain Bay extension.
- Healthcare modernization.
- Digital government acceleration.

## Sectors with active 2024–2026 budget

1. **Financial services (legacy strength)** — banking, Islamic finance, insurance, asset management.
2. **Fintech (priority sector)** — CBB Sandbox, Bahrain FinTech Bay, FinHub 973.
3. **ICT and cloud** — Bahrain became the first GCC government to launch a cloud-first policy in 2017; AWS region operational; Microsoft Azure regional node.
4. **Tourism** — Bahrain Tourism & Exhibitions Authority (BTEA); F1 Grand Prix (annual); culinary tourism.
5. **Manufacturing** — aluminium (Alba — Aluminium Bahrain, one of world's largest single-site smelters); pharmaceuticals; food processing.
6. **Logistics** — King Fahd Causeway to Saudi Arabia + planned second causeway; deepwater Khalifa Bin Salman Port.
7. **Renewable energy** — National Renewable Energy Action Plan (NREAP); 5% renewables by 2025, 20% by 2035.
8. **HealthTech** — Salmaniya Medical Complex modernization.

## Soft-landing programs for foreign founders

### EDB Fast-Track Investor Concierge — FREE

The single best entry path for foreign B2B/B2G founders. EDB provides:
- Pre-arrival market briefing.
- Business registration support (3–5 day CR setup).
- Residency / visa coordination.
- Customer / partner introductions.
- Grant program access (Tamkeen, BDB).
- Office space connections.

### CBB Regulatory Sandbox

For fintech / regtech / Web3:
- 12 months operation runway.
- BHD 100 application fee.
- 15-day review (vs. months elsewhere).
- Rolling intake.
- Foreign + joint applications welcome.

### Tamkeen programs (require Bahraini employment for most)

- 5K Support — BHD 5,000 cap.
- Business Development Program — 50% co-match.
- Raise Program — direct funding.

### NBB Innovation Program

- Accelerator + Demo Day annually.
- $10k cash + 3-month incubation + NBB pilot opportunity.

### Standard Chartered Women in Tech Bahrain

- $35k / $25k / $15k prizes for 1st / 2nd / 3rd.
- 3-month residency.

## What outside founders should know

- Bahrain is the **fastest, cheapest, and most foreign-friendly** GCC entry point — particularly for fintech, financial services, IT, and back-office operations.
- Many founders use Bahrain as a **gateway to Saudi Arabia** — proximity (16km via causeway), shared culture, cheaper operations, easier setup.
- The market is small in absolute terms; Bahrain alone rarely justifies a major commercial push. Best treated as a launchpad + reference customer for the wider GCC.
- The talent pool is more diverse and more cosmopolitan than population size suggests — strong English fluency.

## What NOT to do

- Don't ignore the Bahraini-employee requirement for Tamkeen grants. Plan one local hire from day one.
- Don't underestimate the political sensitivity in Bahrain (Sunni-Shia demographics, regional security context). Stay commercial.
- Don't position Bahrain as your only GCC market — investors will discount you.
