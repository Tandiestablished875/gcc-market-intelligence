# Oman — Country & Cities

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: NCSI (National Centre for Statistics & Information), CBO, OPAZ, Invest Oman, Future Fund Oman, official ministry releases.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

- **Population ~5.0M.** ~57% Omani national, ~43% expat — most heavily Omani-share of GCC excluding Bahrain.
- **GDP 2024 ~$110B.** Hydrocarbon still ~30% of GDP but **Vision 2040 targets reducing this to 16% by 2030, 8.4% by 2040.** Manufacturing target 21% of GDP by 2040.
- **Vision 2040** — implementation tracker shows **74% of indicators with substantial progress** as of 2024–25. Genuine momentum.
- **The cheapest GCC market to enter post-April 2025.** Royal Decree 38/2025 unified SEZ/Free Zone law under OPAZ: **10-year CIT exemption (renewable 2x = up to 30 years), 100% foreign ownership, no minimum capital, 50-year leases.**
- **Future Fund Oman (FFO)** — OIA flagship, **OMR 2B (~$5.2B) over 5 years (2024–28)**. 90% direct projects, 7% SMEs, 3% startups/VC. As of latest reports: **141 projects approved in 2025, $1.2B committed, $2.1B private capital mobilized, 1,400+ jobs created.**
- **Corporate tax: 15%.** VAT: 5%. Personal income tax announced for 2026 (top rate 5% on annual incomes above ~$109k — Oman becoming the first GCC country to introduce PIT). Withholding 10% on payments to non-residents.
- **Omanisation tightened April 1, 2025:** foreign-owned companies must hire ≥1 Omani within 1 year; ICV ≥10% on government tenders.
- **Best market for: deep-tech and infrastructure** given lower buyer concentration than KSA/UAE and lower setup cost.

## Government

- **Sultan:** Haitham bin Tariq Al Said (Al Said dynasty; in power since January 2020 after the death of Sultan Qaboos).
- **Heir Apparent / Crown Prince:** Sayyid Dhi Yazan bin Haitham Al Said (since January 2021; first formal Crown Prince in modern Omani history — Sultan Qaboos had no heir, creating succession uncertainty that drove this constitutional change).

- **CBO Executive President:** Tahir bin Salim Al Amri.

## Key institutions for B2B/B2G founders

- **OIA (Oman Investment Authority)** — sovereign wealth fund ~$53B; 61.3% local focus (more domestic than other GCC SWFs).
- **Future Fund Oman (FFO)** — OIA's flagship economic-diversification vehicle. OMR 2B over 5 years (2024–28).
- **OPAZ (Public Authority for Special Economic Zones & Free Zones)** — unified regulator post-RD 38/2025; manages Duqm, Sohar, Salalah, Al Mazunah free zones + Knowledge Oasis Muscat + others.
- **Invest Oman** — investment promotion platform; under MoCIIP.
- **Madayn (Public Establishment for Industrial Estates)** — manages 14 industrial cities; OMR 245M (~$637M) infrastructure spend planned 2026–30.
- **Oman Technology Fund (OTF)** — $200M fund (established 2016); regional VC.
- **Phaze Ventures** — Oman's first private VC; $30M fund (late 2021); $100k–$500k tickets; seed–Series A; portfolio includes Tarjama, eMushrif, Qeen.ai, SpotLight Earth, CyberOwl (acquired by DNV Oct 2024).
- **Oman Vision 2040 Implementation Follow-up Unit** — quarterly tracking of indicators.

## Cities

### Muscat — capital and political/commercial center

- **Population:** ~1.5M (capital governorate).
- **Districts of commercial relevance:**
  - **Ruwi / CBD** — banking, traditional commercial.
  - **Al Qurum / Shatti Al Qurum** — beachfront commercial / hospitality / retail.
  - **Madinat Al Sultan Qaboos (MQ)** — diplomatic / residential.
  - **Al Khuwair** — government / commercial / hotels.
  - **Knowledge Oasis Muscat (KOM)** — technology park / soft-landing zone.
- **Sectors:** banking, government, tourism, ICT, services.

### Salalah — southern coast, monsoon city

- **Population:** ~420k.
- **Distinct economy:** the only GCC region with a tropical monsoon climate (Khareef, June–September) — major domestic tourism magnet.
- **Salalah Port** — deepwater container transshipment hub; bypass to the Strait of Hormuz; tenants AP Møller-Maersk and others.
- **Salalah Free Zone** — 30-year tax holiday; manufacturing (chemicals, plastics, metal products).

### Sohar — northern industrial port

- **Population:** ~140k city; ~250k+ metropolitan.
- **Sohar Port and Freezone** — joint venture with Port of Rotterdam; 25-year tax holiday; petrochemicals, steel, logistics.
- **Strategic:** outside the Strait of Hormuz; major hydrocarbon export alternative.

### Duqm — the deep-water mega-project

- **Population:** ~50k+ growing rapidly.
- **SEZAD (Special Economic Zone at Duqm)** — at 2,000 km² **the largest free zone in MENA**.
- **Tenants:** BP, Huawei, ACWA Power (Sembcorp solar), Karwa Motors, multiple Chinese-backed industrial projects.
- **Experimental spaceport** — first satellite launch December 2024.
- **Vital for India–Oman strategic relationship** — major Indian investment.
- **Founder relevance:** if you're in heavy industry, energy, logistics, space tech, or large-scale manufacturing — Duqm is the most aggressive incentive package available in the GCC.

### Al Mazunah — Yemen border SEZ

- 30-year tax holiday.
- Border-trade focus; minor relevance to non-trade founders.

## National strategy

### Vision 2040 — what it says

- Oil & gas: 30%+ → 16% by 2030, 8.4% by 2040.
- Manufacturing → 21% of GDP.
- 30% electricity from renewables by 2030.
- Diversification: tourism, logistics, fisheries, mining, ICT, F&B.
- Education: human capital development a top pillar.

### Implementation status (NCSI tracker as of 2024–25)

- **74% of Vision 2040 indicators showing substantial progress.**
- Hydrocarbon share dropping faster than originally projected.
- FDI inflows accelerating post-Royal Decree 38/2025.

## Sectors with active 2024–2026 budget

1. **Renewable energy** — green hydrogen (Hyport Duqm, Hyderabad Industries projects); solar.
2. **Logistics** — Sohar, Salalah, Duqm port modernization.
3. **Mining** — copper, gold, gypsum, chromite, limestone, marble. Vale of Brazil and SAIPEM partnerships.

7. **Manufacturing** — chemicals, plastics, metals, F&B.

## Soft-landing programs for foreign founders

### OPAZ Free Zone licensing (post-RD 38/2025)

The single most attractive package in the GCC:
- 10-year CIT exemption (renewable 2 x 10 yrs = up to 30 years).
- 100% foreign ownership.
- No minimum capital requirement.
- 50-year land lease (renewable).
- Duty-free import of equipment.
- Free profit repatriation.

Choose between:
- **SEZAD (Duqm)** — heavy industry, energy, logistics, space.
- **Sohar Free Zone** — petrochemicals, steel.
- **Salalah Free Zone** — manufacturing.
- **Knowledge Oasis Muscat** — ICT and services.

### Future Fund Oman (FFO)

- Apply via OIA / FFO Direct Project Origination.
- Direct project investments: max OMR 100M each, ≤40% equity.
- SME / startup co-investment funds: IDG Oman Fund ($200M w/ IDG Capital), EWTP Oman Fund ($250M w/ EW Partners).
- 12% target return.

### Phaze Ventures

- Private VC; $100k–$500k seed / Series A tickets.
- Strong B2B/B2G/deep-tech preference.

### Oman Technology Fund (OTF)

- $200M fund; regional VC.

### Invest Oman fast-tracking

- License within 1 day of fee payment for many activities.

## What outside founders should know

- Oman is the **most cost-attractive market entry in the GCC post-April 2025.**
- Buying decisions move slower than Dubai but faster than Riyadh. Less political volatility than Kuwait.
- Family conglomerates dominate distribution; selling B2C or B2B-via-distribution requires partnering with one of them.
- Oman is **strategically positioned outside the Strait of Hormuz** — major value for energy logistics and security-conscious supply chains.
- **PIT introduction in 2026 is a major signal** — Oman is the first GCC state to bite the bullet on income tax. Watch for broader regulatory tightening as fiscal sustainability becomes the priority.

## What NOT to do

- Don't ignore Omanisation enforcement — it tightened in April 2025; ≥1 Omani hire within 1 year for foreign-owned companies.
- Don't underestimate cultural conservatism. Oman is conservative but distinctly Ibadi Muslim — not Sunni-majority like KSA/UAE/Kuwait/Bahrain/Qatar. Politeness, indirectness, and patience matter more.
- Don't market aggressively. Quiet diplomacy works better than loud marketing.
