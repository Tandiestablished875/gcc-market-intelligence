# Kuwait — Country & Kuwait City

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Central Statistical Bureau, Central Bank of Kuwait, KDIPA, Kuwait National Fund for SME Development, official ministry releases.

## TL;DR — 60-second briefing

- **Population ~4.9M.** ~30% Kuwaiti national, ~70% expat — but the political balance favors Kuwaitis disproportionately compared to UAE/Qatar.
- **GDP 2024 ~$163B.** Hydrocarbon-dependent (~60% of GDP, ~90% of exports).
- **Kuwait Vision 2035 ("New Kuwait")** — 5 themes, 7 pillars, 164 strategic programs. Targets: FDI +300%; private-sector employment +69%. **But political-system gridlock has stalled the major megaprojects for over a decade.**
- **Kuwait is the most restrictive GCC market for foreign founders.** Most attractive grants (KNF) are **Kuwaiti-only**. Foreign founders must route through **KDIPA** (Kuwait Direct Investment Promotion Authority).
- **Corporate tax: 15% on foreign-owned profits.** No tax on Kuwaiti companies. No VAT (postponed indefinitely). No personal income tax.
- **KDIPA Direct Investment License** — 100% foreign ownership; up to **10-year CIT exemption** (renewable for another 10); customs/import duty relief; land allocation. Scoring (Resolution 329/2019): tech transfer + Kuwaiti job creation + market dev + sustainability; <30% = rejection; 5/15 = license; 13/15 = full benefits.
- **FY 2024–25 KDIPA approved KD 222.9M (~$725M)** new FDI; cumulative since 2015 = KD 1.97B / 105 entities / 34 countries.
- **Currency: KWD** — highest-valued currency in the world (~$3.25/KWD); pegged to a basket (not USD).
- **Best market for: B2B selling to private-sector buyers** (less government-led than KSA/UAE). The merchant elite is among the wealthiest per-capita globally.

## Key institutions for B2B/B2G founders

- **KDIPA (Kuwait Direct Investment Promotion Authority)** — the foreign-investor agency; license + scoring system. The single most important entity for a non-Kuwaiti founder.
- **KNF (Kuwait National Fund for SME Development)** — KWD 2B (~$6.5B) fund; up to 80% of project capital, max **KWD 500k (~$1.6M)** per project. **Restricted to Kuwaiti citizens / Kuwaiti-led companies** — foreign founders effectively excluded.
- **KIA (Kuwait Investment Authority)** — sovereign wealth fund >$1T; oldest SWF in the world (1953); not generally a venture investor in early-stage Western tech.
- **Kuwait Foundation for the Advancement of Sciences (KFAS)** — research and innovation grant body.
- **Central Bank of Kuwait** — runs Fintech Regulatory Sandbox.
- **CITRA (Communication & Information Technology Regulatory Authority)** — telecom + cloud + data.

## National strategy

### Kuwait Vision 2035 / "New Kuwait" — what it says vs what's happened

Five themes (effective public sector, sustainable economy, attractive living environment, developed infrastructure, prominent international position) and seven pillars. 164 strategic programs.

**Headline megaprojects (most stalled politically):**
- **Madinat Al-Hareer ("Silk City")** + **Burj Mubarak Al Kabir Tower** (1,000m, would be tallest in the world) — northern Kuwait; long-stalled politically.
- **Bubiyan Island** — northern logistics megaproject including Mubarak Al Kabeer Port; long-stalled.

**Foreign founder reality check:** as of early 2026 the post-2024 political reorganization may unblock some of these, but execution risk remains the highest in the GCC. Don't price your model around contracts on these projects until they actually start procuring.

1. **Banking & financial services** — strong private sector; large Islamic banking sector.
2. **Hydrocarbon services & technology** — KPC (Kuwait Petroleum Corporation) and its subsidiaries (KOC, KNPC, KIPIC).
3. **Healthcare** — public + private hospitals (Dasman Diabetes Institute, Sabah Health Region).
4. **Retail / F&B / hospitality** — concentrated in private family hands (Alshaya, Alghanim).

6. **Logistics** — port modernization (Mubarak Al Kabeer Port if Bubiyan progresses).
7. **EdTech / digital learning** — strong post-COVID adoption.
8. **Fintech and digital payments** — CBK Sandbox, KFH and major private bank pushes.
9. **Energy efficiency / renewables** — limited but growing.

See `references/families/other-gcc-families.md` for detail. Kuwait's private sector is unusually concentrated in family conglomerates that often outshine government as customer:

- **MA Kharafi Group** — engineering, contracting, telecom (founding shareholder Zain Group); multi-billion.
- **Alghanim Industries** — GM dealer for Kuwait, consumer electronics (XCite), F&B, finance.
- **Al Sayer Group** — Toyota / Lexus dealer for Kuwait; one of the oldest Toyota franchises globally.
- **Alshaya Group** — operates 9 countries / 90+ brands (Starbucks, H&M, Mothercare, Bath & Body Works); Kuwait-based franchise giant.
- **Al Bahar** — Caterpillar dealer for GCC.
- **Behbehani Brothers** — Mercedes-Benz, BMW dealer; diversified.
- **Al Mulla Group** — auto, finance, retail.
- **Boodai Corporation** — aviation (Jazeera Airways), diversified.

These families are wealthy, sophisticated, decision-fast (private = no government bureaucracy), and have pan-GCC reach.

## Soft-landing programs for foreign founders

### KDIPA Direct Investment License

The path to 10-year tax exemption + 100% ownership. Process:
1. Submit application to KDIPA (~6–12 weeks review).
2. Scoring on Resolution 329/2019 criteria:
   - Tech / knowledge transfer
   - Kuwaiti job creation
   - Market development
   - Sustainability
3. <30% score = rejected. 5/15 = license only. 13/15 = full incentives (10-year tax exemption, customs relief, land).
4. Land allocation via KDIPA → can be in industrial zones (Shuaiba, Mina Abdullah, Shadadiya) or commercial.

### Central Bank of Kuwait Regulatory Sandbox

For fintech / regtech. Similar structure to CBB Bahrain sandbox but more selective.

### KFAS Research Grants

For deep-tech with Kuwait research-university collaboration (Kuwait University, Kuwait Institute for Scientific Research).

## What outside founders should know

- **Kuwait is best approached LAST in a GCC sequence** — after you have references in KSA, UAE, or both. Kuwaiti decision-makers respect existing regional credibility.
- The merchant elite is the buying center. Pitch family groups before government.
- KDIPA license is paperwork-heavy but worth it for the 10-year tax exemption.
- Avoid setting up if you don't plan to actively serve the Kuwait market — costs (KWD-denominated) are not low.
- Cultural register is more conservative than UAE, less than KSA. Strict on alcohol enforcement (no licensed alcohol since 1965).

## What NOT to do

- Don't pitch KNF as a foreign founder — you're not eligible.
- Don't underestimate political volatility. Kuwait has dissolved Parliament more times than any other GCC state.
- Don't assume the megaprojects will start on schedule.
- Don't overlook the family conglomerates. They are the actual market.
