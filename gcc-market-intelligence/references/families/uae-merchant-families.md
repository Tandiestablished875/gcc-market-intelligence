# UAE Merchant Families — the Old-Money Conglomerates

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Forbes Middle East rich list (Feb 2025), Forbes Top 100 Arab Family Businesses, KPMG *Regenerative power of family businesses: UAE edition* (Oct 2022), DFM / ADX filings, company press. Net worth figures shift; verify before pitching.

## Why this matters to a founder

UAE family-owned entities represent ~90% of private companies and ~60% of GDP (KPMG 2022). They dominate distribution (auto, retail, FMCG), construction, hospitality, services. Even where they don't own outright, they hold the local franchise / sponsorship / distribution rights.

The fundamental fact: foreign brands — IKEA, M&S, Carrefour, Toyota, BMW, Mercedes, McDonald's, Starbucks, Hertz, Avis, Marriott, Hilton, Waldorf Astoria — typically operate through one of these families. So do enterprise tech buyers (the families' own infrastructure spend is often 8-figure annually).

## Tier 1 — the largest commercial groups

### Al Futtaim Group (Abdulla Al Futtaim branch)

- **Founder:** Majid Al Futtaim's father split the family in 2000; **Abdulla Al Futtaim** got the auto / retail / financial-services side; Majid got the malls / retail-investment side. These are now two separate empires.
- **Now run by:** Abdulla Al Futtaim and son Omar Al Futtaim.
- **Net worth (Forbes Feb 2025):** Abdulla Al Futtaim ~$2.9B.
- **Headline holdings:** 200+ businesses; 33,000 staff in 20 countries. **Toyota and Lexus exclusive UAE distribution.** Franchises: **IKEA** (UAE, Qatar, Oman, Egypt), **Marks & Spencer**, **Zara** (UAE), Banana Republic, Plaza Premium Group (airport lounges). Al-Futtaim Engineering & Technologies (large UAE SI).
- **For founders:** if you need automotive distribution, retail / mall presence, or services into Toyota dealerships — this is the door.

### Majid Al Futtaim Holding (MAF) (Majid Al Futtaim branch)

- **Founder:** Majid Al Futtaim (d. 2021). Now professionally run; non-family CEO Ahmed Galal Ismail.
- **FY 2025 results:** AED 35.9B (~$9.8B) consolidated revenue; EBITDA AED 5.1B; 29 shopping malls (98% occupancy); 43,000+ employees.
- **Headline holdings:** Mall of the Emirates, Mall of Egypt, City Centre malls (many countries); **Carrefour MENA franchise**; VOX Cinemas; Magic Planet; Ski Dubai; Najm finance.
- **For founders:** if you sell retail-tech, customer analytics, omnichannel, or B2C anywhere a Carrefour or City Centre mall exists in MENA — MAF is the buyer or the channel.

### Al Ghurair Group

- **Founder:** the Al Ghurair family (Saif + Abdullah branches).
- **Family net worth (Forbes Feb 2025):** ~$4.7B–$4.9B combined.
- **Headline holdings:** **Mashreqbank** — ~70% combined Saif + Abdullah equity stake. **Al Ghurair Foods** (one of the largest mill operators in MENA), **Al Ghurair Construction** (Burj Khalifa cladding, Dubai Metro construction), real estate.
- **For founders:** food / FMCG, banking / fintech (via Mashreq), and infrastructure construction. Mashreq Neo is a notable fintech experiment.

### Al Habtoor Group

- **Founder:** Khalaf Al Habtoor; now Mohammed Al Habtoor (CEO).
- **Mohammed Al Habtoor net worth (Forbes Feb 2025):** ~$1.5B.
- **Headline holdings:** Hospitality (Habtoor Palace Dubai, Habtoor Grand, **Waldorf Astoria Palm Jumeirah**); Auto (Mitsubishi, Bentley, Bugatti, McLaren); Education (Emirates International School); Habtoor Polo Resort.

### Al Naboodah Group

- **Founder:** Saeed bin Ahmed Al Naboodah (1958).
- **Headline holdings:** ~14,000 employees. Built Palm Jumeirah, Yas Island, DXB airport, Expo 2020 infrastructure. Auto: Peugeot, Citroen, Volvo, Honda Trucks (UAE).
- **For founders:** construction tech, infrastructure, fleet management.

## Generational dynamics — succession and direct VC

UAE family groups are professionalizing rapidly:
- Many top-tier groups (Chalhoub, MAF, Al Futtaim) now run with non-family CEOs.
- Younger heirs (Omar Al Futtaim, Hassan Al Habtoor, Nadia Al Tayer, etc.) are leading direct VC investment activity.
- New entrants: **IHC, Multiply Group, ADQ, Mubadala Capital** — while ruling-family-aligned, they collectively are reshaping the venture / growth-stage market by offering check sizes that match Tier 1 family offices.

## What outside founders should know

- **Distribution rights are a moat.** If you're selling a physical or licensed product into UAE retail or auto, you almost certainly need a deal with one of these families. The major franchise / distribution rights are exclusive and renew long.
- **Pitch the chairman's office only via warm intro.** Cold emails to chairman@ have ~0% conversion. The actual decision-maker is often a Senior VP, Group Strategy, or a family-office investment director.
- **Most UAE family offices today operate in venture / growth mode.** $250k–$10M checks are routine; bigger via co-investment with Mubadala / IHC / ADQ.
- **English is the working language** across nearly all UAE family offices.

## What NOT to do

- Don't conflate Al Futtaim and Majid Al Futtaim. They are separate empires.
- Don't gossip about family disputes (Al Gurg succession, Al Habtoor branches, etc.) — word travels.
- Don't oversell. Conservative numbers earn more credibility than aggressive ones with these audiences.
- Don't pitch family offices a deal you're simultaneously shopping to their competitors in the same emirate — they talk.
