# Saudi Merchant Families — the Non-Royal Commercial Aristocracy

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Forbes Top 100 Arab Family Businesses 2022–24, Forbes Middle East rich lists, Tadawul filings, company press. Ownership stakes and succession statuses move; verify before serious engagement.

## Why this matters to a founder

Roughly 200 family-owned conglomerates control the bulk of Saudi private-sector commerce. They are:
- The largest non-government B2B buyers in the Kingdom.
- The dominant distributors / franchisees for foreign brands (auto, retail, F&B, pharma).
- Increasingly active as direct investors into venture-stage and growth-stage companies (generational shift from arms-length finance to operator/VC mode in 2024–25).

You can sell into Saudi without ever meeting a royal. You cannot sell into Saudi without meeting at least one major merchant family.

## The top tier (Forbes Top 5 Arab Family Businesses, repeated rankings 2022–24)

### Olayan Financing Company (OFC) / Olayan Saudi Holding Company (OSHCO)

- **Founded:** 1947 by Sheikh Suliman Saleh Olayan.
- **Now run by:** Lubna Olayan (Chair OSHCO; chairs Saudi British Bank/SABB until 2024). Multi-generational professionalized.
- **Sectors:** energy services, ICT, F&B, restaurants, real estate, distribution.
- **Headline holdings:** Coca-Cola Saudi bottler; historical ~18% SABB stake; international real estate including 550 Madison NYC and Hotel Ritz Madrid. ~14,000+ staff.
- **For founders:** sophisticated, professionally run, slow to commit but powerful when they do. Strong corporate-governance posture. Good for partnership / distribution deals at scale.

### Al Muhaidib Group

- **Founded:** 1943.
- **Chair:** Sulaiman Al Muhaidib.
- **Sectors:** 200+ companies. Food distribution (Savola Group — Al Muhaidib holds ~8.2%), construction (Masdar; 105 branches), real estate (Rafal), energy (ACWA Power stake).
- **For founders:** very active acquirer; routes via Al Muhaidib Family Office for VC-style and growth deals. Practical decision-making.

### Al Rajhi (the banking branch)

- **Founder:** Sheikh Sulaiman Al Rajhi (1929–2023).
- **Headline asset:** **Al Rajhi Bank** — the world's largest Islamic bank by share capital (~$4.3B). Listed on Tadawul.
- **Other branches:** Yazeed Al Rajhi runs **Naif Al Rajhi Investment**; 2023 announcement of $3B+ steel JV with PIF + SABIC. Multiple Al Rajhi cousins run separate businesses; the surname does not imply unified ownership.
- **For founders:** if you sell to or compete with Saudi banks, Al Rajhi Bank is core. The investment arms (e.g. Naif Al Rajhi Investment) actively co-invest with PIF and operate at speed.

### Rashed Abdul Rahman Al Rashed & Sons

- **Founded:** Eastern Province family.
- **Chair:** Abdulaziz Al Rashed — also chairs Riyad Bank.
- **Sectors:** 26 wholly-owned companies; construction, real estate, manufacturing, distribution.
- **For founders:** powerful Eastern Province presence. If you sell to Aramco vendors or Eastern Province industrial buyers, this name comes up repeatedly.

### Abdul Latif Jameel (ALJ)

- **Founder:** Abdul Latif Jameel; family run by Mohammed Jameel + Hassan Jameel.
- **Headline holdings:** Toyota distributor in 30 countries (one of the largest non-Japanese Toyota franchises globally). FRV (Fotowatio Renewable Ventures — major solar IPP, sold majority to ACWA). Community Jameel (philanthropy / impact). Significant impact-investing portfolio.
- **For founders:** ALJ is one of the most internationalized Saudi groups. Sophisticated, English-fluent leadership. Open to co-investments and partnerships in mobility, renewables, social impact.

## The second tier — major but specialized

### Saudi Binladin Group (SBG)

- **Founder:** Mohammed bin Awad bin Laden.
- **Sector:** construction — the historical builder of the Two Holy Mosques (Mecca and Medina). After 2017 reorganization, PIF holds a controlling stake. Reconstituted as a more state-aligned entity.
- **For founders:** if your product touches Hajj/Umrah infrastructure, holy-sites construction, or large-scale Saudi infrastructure, SBG is the channel partner conversation.

### Zamil Group

- **Sectors:** steel structures (Zamil Steel — major regional manufacturer), industrial, real estate, services.
- **For founders:** strong industrial / manufacturing alignment. Eastern Province + national.

### Al Faisaliah Group

- **Linked to:** the late Prince Mohammed bin Faisal Al Saud branch (royal-merchant overlap).
- **Sectors:** Al Faisaliah Medical Systems, Sabb Takaful, Rolex distribution, retail (carrefour-Saudi historical), property.
- **For founders:** moderate-pace. Good if you have a medical-systems or premium-retail angle.

### Y.M.A. Naghi (Naghi Brothers)

- **Sectors:** exclusive Saudi distributor for Rolls-Royce and BMW.
- **For founders:** mostly relevant if you sell into luxury automotive supply chain or after-sales service tech.

### Al Othaim Holding (Abdullah Al Othaim Markets)

- **Sectors:** food retail (Al Othaim Markets — major listed retailer), real estate, entertainment (Al Othaim Investment listed on Tadawul; Al Othaim Markets listed separately).
- **For founders:** if you sell retail-tech, supply chain, or F&B distribution tech, Al Othaim is a key target. Strong Jeddah + Riyadh footprint.

### Al Nahla Group (Sharbatly family)

- **Headline holding:** historic ~8.74% in Riyad Bank.
- **Sectors:** diversified holding; auto, F&B, real estate.

### SEDCO Holding

- **Founder:** Saleh Abdullah Kamel (d. 2020).
- **Sectors:** Islamic finance, media (the old Dallah AlBaraka roots), real estate.
- **For founders:** culturally important — sometimes the door into the Islamic-finance establishment.

### Tamer Group

- **Founded:** 1922 — Saudi Arabia's first pharmacy.
- **Sectors:** pharmaceutical distribution, healthcare products, beauty.
- **For founders:** essential channel partner if you're in pharma / consumer healthcare / cosmetics distribution.

### BinDawood Holding

- **Sectors:** food retail (BinDawood Markets, Danube). Listed on Tadawul.
- **For founders:** F&B / FMCG / retail tech.

### Nahdi Medical Company

- **Sectors:** pharmacy retail — largest pharmacy chain in MENA. Listed on Tadawul.
- **For founders:** healthtech / pharmacy automation / consumer-health distribution.

## Generational shift — important context

Wamda series 2024–25 documented a substantial generational shift: younger heirs (40s and below) increasingly pivot from passive shareholding to:
- **Direct startup investing** — Al Muhaidib Family Office, Naif Al Rajhi Investment, Jameel Investment Management Co. (JIMCO) running global VC/PE portfolios.
- **Venture studios** — building rather than just funding.
- **Deep-tech and ESG/impact bias** — particularly in healthtech, climatetech, AI, mobility.

The implication for foreign founders: the family-office route is more open in 2025–26 than at any previous point. But the diligence bar is also higher — you are pitching investment professionals, not patriarchs.

## What outside founders should know

- **Warm intros are non-negotiable.** Cold emails to a Saudi family office have ~0% reply rate. Get a portfolio company founder, a banker, or a fellow LP to introduce you.
- **The first meeting is almost never the deal meeting.** Build trust over 2–4 meetings, then expect a 12–18 month commitment cycle for SWF-style family offices, faster for direct merchant family deals.
- **Don't conflate the family with its operating companies.** Pitch the right CEO or investment director — not the patriarch. The patriarch hears about you later.
- **English is fine for most family offices.** Bilingual decks impress but aren't required. Knowing how to pronounce family names correctly is required.

## What NOT to do

- Don't gossip about other families. Word travels in days.
- Don't reveal what one family told you to another family. Confidentiality is a reputational currency.
- Don't try to negotiate hard on a first deal — early deals are relationship investments, not financial optimization.
- Don't bring up family disputes, successions, or inheritance battles. Not your topic.
