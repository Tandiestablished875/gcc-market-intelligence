# Other GCC Families — Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Forbes Middle East, Bloomberg, regional press, company disclosures. Family-business holdings change; verify before pitching at scale.

## Why a single file

These four countries have smaller commercial ecosystems than KSA or UAE; the family universe is correspondingly smaller. Grouping them here is operationally efficient — most foreign founders meeting a Qatari family will also meet a Bahraini family within the same trip.

## Qatar

### Al Mannai Corporation

- Long-established Qatari trading and industrial group. Multi-generational; one of the oldest Doha-based conglomerates.
- **Sectors:** automotive (multiple distributorships historically), engineering, industrial supplies, real estate.
- **Founder relevance:** B2B distribution, industrial supply chains.

### Al Faisal Holding

- **Founder/Chair:** Sheikh Faisal bin Qassim Al Thani (royal-linked but operates as merchant conglomerate).
- **Sectors:** real estate (Aamal Company — listed on Qatar Exchange), aviation (Al Maha Services), hospitality (Kempinski Doha and others), education (Al Faisal International Academy).
- **Founder relevance:** real estate / hospitality channel partnerships.

### Power Holding International (PHI)

- Sheikh Faisal Al Thani's energy and industrial arm.
- **Sectors:** power, water, infrastructure.

### Al Darwish Holding (Darwish family)

- One of the original Huwala merchant families (Sunni Iranian-origin Gulf merchants who settled in Qatar / Bahrain / UAE historically).
- **Sectors:** diversified — trading, real estate, retail.

### Al Fardan Group

- **Sectors:** exchange / remittance (Al Fardan Exchange — large regional remittance player), jewellery, real estate. Operates in both **Qatar and Bahrain**.
- **Founder relevance:** fintech / remittance ecosystem.

### Al Jaber Group

- **Sectors:** construction, engineering, services. Major contractor for Qatari infrastructure.
- **Founder relevance:** construction tech, project management.

## Bahrain

Bahrain's family ecosystem is smaller in count but deep in history (the oldest GCC trading houses).

### YBA Kanoo

- **Founded:** 1890. One of the oldest Gulf trading houses; pan-GCC operations.
- **Sectors:** shipping & logistics, travel (Kanoo Travel — IATA-accredited; major travel agent across GCC), industrial / oil services, real estate.
- **Founder relevance:** logistics, travel tech, B2B services pan-GCC.

### Al Zayani Group

- **Sectors:** automotive (BMW Bahrain), pharmaceuticals, real estate, investments.
- **Founder relevance:** premium auto, healthcare distribution.

### Mohammed Jalal Group

- **Sectors:** diversified — F&B, automotive (Honda, others historically), electronics, real estate, construction.
- **Founder relevance:** retail / F&B / auto distribution.

### Yousif Khalil Almoayyed & Sons

- **Sectors:** automotive (Nissan, Renault, others), electronics, hardware, construction, services.

### Mohammed Hasan Kanoo (MHK)

- Separate from YBA Kanoo branch. Diversified holding — industrial, real estate, trade.

### Al Fardan Group (also see Qatar)

- Same family; operates in both Bahrain and Qatar.

### Banader Hotels Company

- Listed on Bahrain Bourse; hospitality.

## Kuwait — family conglomerates matter MORE than government

Kuwait's private sector is unusually concentrated in family conglomerates that often outshine government as customer. Their decision speed (private = no government bureaucracy) and pan-GCC reach make them the actual market for many B2B products.

### M.A. Kharafi Group (Kharafi National)

- **Founder:** Mohammed Abdulmohsin Al-Kharafi (d. 2011).
- **Sectors:** engineering, contracting, F&B (Americana Restaurants — KFC, Pizza Hut, Hardee's, TGI Friday's across MENA; majority sold to ADQ/Adeptio 2017), telecom (founding shareholder of Zain Group), real estate.
- **Founder relevance:** the single largest private buyer in Kuwait by ticket size for engineering / infrastructure.

### Alghanim Industries

- **Founder:** the late Yusuf Ahmed Alghanim. Now led by Omar Alghanim.
- **Sectors:** **GM dealer for Kuwait** (Chevrolet, Cadillac, GMC); consumer electronics retail (**XCite Electronics**); F&B (Costa Coffee, Wendy's, Kuwait); finance (Gulf Bank stake); insurance.
- **Founder relevance:** retail tech, auto dealership tech, B2B fintech.

### Al Sayer Group

- **Sectors:** **Toyota / Lexus dealer for Kuwait** — one of the oldest Toyota franchises globally (since 1954). Diversified — auto, real estate, F&B.
- **Founder relevance:** automotive ecosystem, dealership tech.

### Alshaya Group (Kuwaiti-origin, pan-GCC operator)

- **Founder:** the late Mohammed Hamad Alshaya (d. 2014); now run by Mohammed Alshaya (son).
- **Headline holdings:** operates 90+ brands across 9 countries: **Starbucks**, **H&M**, **Mothercare**, **Bath & Body Works**, **Boots**, **The Body Shop**, **American Eagle**, Victoria's Secret, Pottery Barn, **The Cheesecake Factory**, Pinkberry, Texas Roadhouse, and many more.
- **Founder relevance:** if you sell retail-tech, F&B operations tech, supply chain, or customer analytics to any major mall in MENA — Alshaya is the buyer.

### Al Bahar (Mohamed Abdulrahman Al Bahar)

- **Sectors:** **Caterpillar dealer for the entire GCC**. Construction equipment, mining equipment, power generation.
- **Founder relevance:** heavy equipment, fleet management, mining services.

### Behbehani Brothers

- **Sectors:** Mercedes-Benz, BMW dealer Kuwait; diversified — auto, real estate, services.

### Al Mulla Group

- **Sectors:** auto (Hyundai, Mitsubishi historically), finance, retail (multiple QSR brands historically), media.

### Boodai Corporation

- **Sectors:** aviation (**Jazeera Airways**), diversified industrial, real estate, F&B.
- **Founder relevance:** aviation services, MRO.

## Cross-country observations

- **Most foreign founders meeting one of these families will meet several within a single trip.** Plan trips with a 3–5 meeting cadence per day during the active season (Sep–Nov, Jan–Mar).
- **Pan-GCC families (Kanoo, Al Fardan, Alshaya, Bahwan) are decision-fast** because they have already operationalized cross-border governance. Smaller country-specific families are slower.
- **Auto distribution as a proxy:** the auto dealership family in each country is usually one of the top 3 commercial actors. Knowing them is a cheap intelligence shortcut.

## What outside founders should know

- These families collectively buy 6–9 figure annual enterprise tech (ERP, CRM, data, supply chain, payments) — a real B2B market beyond the obvious franchise-distribution business.
- Several have **internal corporate VC arms** — pitch the family office, not just the operating company.
- **Bilingual is appreciated, English is fine.** Family principals typically educated US / UK / Lebanon / Egypt.
- For Bahrain and Oman: family-group decision-makers are usually directly accessible after one warm intro. The geographies are small enough to scale faster than KSA/UAE.

## What NOT to do

- Don't assume Qatar's Al Thani-linked merchant entities (Al Faisal Holding, PHI) operate identically to other Qatari merchant families. The royal overlay changes dynamics.
- Don't pitch Kuwaiti merchant families in August. Most are in Europe.
- Don't be the third foreign founder pitching the same family the same week in a small market like Bahrain. Coordinate, or wait.
- Don't assume Khimji Ramdas operates like a traditional Arab family — they are a Sindhi-origin Omani family. Cultural protocol overlaps but has distinct features.
