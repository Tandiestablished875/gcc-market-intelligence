# Al Saud — Saudi Ruling Family

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Royal Court releases, MISA, PIF, official biographies. Senior appointments shift; verify before any high-stakes pitch.

## Why this matters to a founder

The Al Saud is not just a ruling family — it operates as both the political authority AND a set of major commercial actors. Some of the largest deals in the Kingdom run through investment vehicles linked to specific princes. Knowing which branch holds which portfolio cuts months off your relationship-mapping.

Two operating realities to internalize:
1. **Power is concentrated in MBS and the Salman branch (the Sudairis).** The big buyers (PIF, NEOM, Aramco, MiSK) report through him.
2. **Other senior princes still control regional governorates and specific portfolios.** Don't ignore them — they unlock entire regions.

## Senior figures and their commercial significance

### The King

- **King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud** (b. 1935, on throne since Jan 2015). Largely symbolic in 2025–26; day-to-day governance delegated to Crown Prince. Former Governor of Riyadh Province for 48 years (1963–2011) — that network still matters for any Riyadh-centric pitch.

### The Crown Prince (MBS) and his portfolio

- **Crown Prince & Prime Minister: Mohammed bin Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud (MBS)** (b. 1985). Chairs Council of Economic and Development Affairs (CEDA), chairs PIF, chairs Council of Political and Security Affairs. De facto decision-maker on every major economic policy and capital deployment.
- **MiSK Foundation** — MBS's personal philanthropy/talent vehicle. Funds MiSK Innovation, MiSK Schools, the Misk-Tencent partnership, and the Misk Foundation Fellowship. Genuinely a soft-landing pathway for young Saudi talent and select foreign founders.
- **Public Investment Fund (PIF)** — chaired by MBS; AUM ~$1.15T; ~700+ portfolio companies; the most important single buyer in Saudi Arabia. See `references/sovereign-funds.md`.

### Other senior princes who matter to founders

- **Prince Abdulaziz bin Salman Al Saud** — Minister of Energy. Decision-making power over hydrogen, renewables, Aramco-adjacent energy contracts. Half-brother of MBS.
- **Prince Faisal bin Farhan Al Saud** — Minister of Foreign Affairs. Relevant for ambassadors, diplomatic facilitation, multilateral deals.
- **Prince Khalid bin Salman Al Saud** — Minister of Defense. Defense procurement gateway (note: most defense procurement is OUTSIDE Etimad via GAMI/SAMI).
- **Prince Turki bin Mohammed Al Saud** — Adviser at the Royal Court; key cultural / soft-power portfolio.
- **Prince Sultan bin Salman Al Saud** — first Arab astronaut; previously chaired Saudi Commission for Tourism and Heritage; still influential in heritage / aviation circles.

### Regional governors (heaviest commercial gatekeepers)

- **Riyadh Province: Prince Faisal bin Bandar bin Abdulaziz Al Saud** (Governor since 2015). Riyadh Municipality and Royal Commission for Riyadh City (RCRC) sit under his oversight. Chairs RCRC.
- **Makkah Province: Prince Khalid Al Faisal bin Abdulaziz Al Saud** (returned as Governor; senior royal poet/intellectual). Oversight of Hajj/Umrah infrastructure spending.
- **Madinah Province: Prince Salman bin Sultan bin Abdulaziz Al Saud** (Governor; royal nephew). Oversight of Madinah Tech Cultivator alignment.
- **Eastern Province: Prince Saud bin Naif bin Abdulaziz Al Saud** (Governor; ARAMCO heartland). Critical gatekeeper for petrochem / oil-services sales.
- **Tabuk Province: Prince Fahd bin Sultan bin Abdulaziz Al Saud**. Tabuk = NEOM region.

## Commercial vehicles linked to specific princes

These are NOT government bodies — they are private or quasi-private investment vehicles controlled by named princes. Confusing them with PIF or government ministries is a common foreign-founder mistake.

### Kingdom Holding Company (KHC)

- **Founder & Chair: Prince Alwaleed bin Talal bin Abdulaziz Al Saud** ("the Talal branch" — separate political line from MBS). Listed on Tadawul; PIF acquired 16.87% in May 2022 for SAR 6B. Investments globally (Citigroup, Twitter/X, Snapchat, Lyft, Uber, Four Seasons, hotels). Major real estate in KSA (Kingdom Tower, Kingdom Center).
- **For founders:** Kingdom Holding is approachable via Kingdom Investments arm. Decision speed is faster than PIF. Investment focus: hospitality, tech, financial services.

### Sheikh Sultan Holding Group / Al Sultan branch

- Various princes manage personal investment offices. These are smaller in scale than KHC but can move quickly on ticket sizes of $1M–$20M.

### Royal Commission entities (technically government but staffed by royals + technocrats)

- **Royal Commission for Riyadh City (RCRC)** — major procurement body for Riyadh; chaired by Riyadh Governor.
- **Royal Commission for AlUla (RCU)** — heritage tourism; CEO previously Amr AlMadani; major procurement for cultural tech, hospitality, sustainability.
- **Royal Commission for Jubail and Yanbu (RCJY)** — industrial cities authority; procurement for petrochem, manufacturing, infrastructure.

## What outside founders actually need to do

1. **Identify the right branch first.** Is your buyer (a) MBS/PIF-side, (b) Talal/Kingdom Holding-side, or (c) a regional governor's portfolio? These are different doors.
2. **Don't try to pitch a senior royal directly in a first meeting.** Pitch the named CEO or VP of the relevant entity. Royal-level conversations come later, if at all.
3. **The MiSK Foundation and Misk Innovation are the most accessible entry points** for foreign founders that have any alignment with talent / education / youth innovation. Different category from PIF.
4. **Family disputes are real but not your concern.** Don't try to navigate them. Stay on commercial topics.

## What NOT to do

- Don't use the phrase "the royal family" in conversation with Saudi counterparts. Use "His Royal Highness," "the Crown Prince," or specific names.
- Don't bring up the Khashoggi case, the 2017 Ritz-Carlton corruption purge, or detained dissidents. Not your topic.
- Don't show photographs of yourself with royals on social media unless you have explicit permission — it is socially read as boasting and reflects badly.
- Don't assume that meeting one prince warms you up to others. Branches operate independently.
