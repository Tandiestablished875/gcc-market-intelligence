# Saudi Government Procurement — Etimad

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Etimad portal, Ministry of Finance, GTPL (Government Tenders and Procurement Law), RHQ regulations.

## TL;DR

- **Etimad** (https://tenders.etimad.sa) is the central government procurement portal, launched 2018 by the Ministry of Finance. All Saudi government procurement runs through here.
- **Two notable exceptions:** healthcare procurement (National Unified Procurement Company — NUPCO) and defense procurement (General Authority for Military Industries — GAMI, plus SAMI as buyer).
- **RHQ Program is binding since January 1, 2024.** MNCs without a Regional HQ license **cannot win** Saudi government tenders, with limited exemptions.
- **Foreign suppliers can register** but must provide a professional practice license OR engage a Saudi agent.
- **10% price preference for GCC-origin products** in tender evaluation.
- **Annual subscription fee:** SAR 1,500.

## How Etimad works (step by step)

1. **Government agency identifies need** and issues tender documentation on Etimad.
2. **Publication** — by GTPL, public tenders must be announced in Umm al-Qoura gazette + 2 local newspapers + electronic media (Etimad satisfies the electronic media requirement).
3. **Pre-qualification / supplier registration** — vendors register on Etimad.
4. **Bid submission** — usually 30–60 days bid window for standard tenders.
5. **Bid opening** — sealed-bid format; technical and commercial separately.
6. **Evaluation** — each agency has its own contracting authority and evaluation committee. ICV / GCC-origin preferences applied.
7. **Award** — public on Etimad.
8. **Contract** — signed; performance bond required.
9. **Payment** — typically 60–90 days for government; longer in some cases.

## RHQ Program — the gating regulation

### What it is

The **Regional Headquarters Program (Jan 1, 2024)** mandates that MNCs seeking to win Saudi government tenders must have a Regional HQ licensed in Saudi Arabia.

### Year-one compliance obligations

- ≥15 FTE in year one.
- ≥3 C-suite level resident in KSA.
- Mandatory regional activities (treasury, business strategy, M&A oversight, P&L for region) operational within 6 months.
- Quarterly board meetings physically in KSA.

### Incentives (substantial)

- **0% Corporate Income Tax** on RHQ qualifying activities for 30 years.
- **0% withholding tax** on RHQ qualifying activities for 30 years.
- **10-year Saudization exemption** (a major operational concession).
- **Unlimited employee visas** subject to RHQ scope.

### Penalties

- SAR 100k for missed obligations.
- SAR 400k for non-operational RHQ.
- License revocation for ongoing non-compliance.

### Recent change

In 2026, a **carve-out provision** allows government to contract a non-RHQ company if (a) no RHQ-qualified bidder exists, OR (b) the non-RHQ bid is technically best AND ≥25% cheaper than the next RHQ-qualified bid. Verify current carve-out wording with MISA; this provision has been refined multiple times.

### Current RHQ landscape

By end-2025: **700+ MNCs licensed** with RHQ status (original target was 500 by 2030; overshot 4 years early).

### What this means for a Series A founder

- If you're early-stage and not yet ready to commit to ≥15 KSA FTE, **partner with an RHQ-licensed SI or distributor** for government work — see `references/system-integrators.md`.
- If you have $5M+ to commit to a KSA presence and your TAM includes government work — apply for RHQ. The 30-year tax incentives make it a very strong package.
- The IRC (Investment Registration Certificate, post-Feb 2025) is a separate, faster license that gives you mainland operation without RHQ — but won't unlock government tenders.

## Foreign supplier registration

Two paths:
1. **Direct foreign supplier registration** with a professional practice license (MISA license + relevant sector license).
2. **Via a Saudi agent / sponsor** who carries the prime contract.

Annual subscription on Etimad: SAR 1,500.

### Documents typically required
- MISA license / IRC.
- Tax certificate (ZATCA).
- Saudization certificate (Qiwa).
- ICV (Local Content) score where applicable.
- Audited financial statements (3 years).
- Bank guarantees / performance bonds capability evidence.

## ICV / Local Content scoring

Saudi has been progressively tightening **Local Content** preferences in tender evaluation:

- **GCC-origin products**: 10% price preference in evaluation.
- **Saudi-origin products**: additional preference under "Saudi Made" program.
- **Local Content score**: factored into technical evaluation in many ministries, particularly Aramco, SABIC, PIF portfolio companies (these have their own ICV/Local Content frameworks separate from Etimad-direct).

Different from UAE ICV (which is a federal certification with specific Tier scoring). Always verify the specific scoring rubric for your tendering entity.

## Sectoral exceptions to Etimad

### Healthcare — NUPCO

The **National Unified Procurement Company (NUPCO)** runs centralized procurement for Ministry of Health, Saudi Red Crescent, military medical services, and other government healthcare providers. NUPCO operates separately from Etimad with its own supplier registration and bidding portal.

- **Verify:** https://www.nupco.com.

### Defense — GAMI + SAMI

The **General Authority for Military Industries (GAMI)** is the regulator and procurement authority for defense.

The **Saudi Arabian Military Industries (SAMI)** is the state-owned defense systems integrator — both buyer and competitor depending on your product.

- **Verify:** https://www.gami.gov.sa.

### Energy — Aramco supplier registration

**Saudi Aramco** has its own supplier qualification system separate from Etimad. The IKtva (In-Kingdom Total Value Add) program is Aramco's localization scoring framework.

- **Verify:** https://www.aramco.com/en/working-with-us.

### PIF portfolio companies — separate procurement

Most PIF subsidiaries (NEOM, Red Sea Global, Diriyah, Qiddiya, ROSHN, etc.) run procurement through their own internal teams, sometimes via Etimad and sometimes outside. Sales motion for these is enterprise-style, not tender-only.

## Tender history as competitive intel

The single most underused founder tool is **Etimad's public tender history filter**:

1. Go to https://tenders.etimad.sa.
2. Search by sector or category.
3. Filter by closed / awarded tenders in the last 24–36 months.
4. Identify winning suppliers + price points + ministries.
5. Build your competitive map — see `references/system-integrators.md`.

Paid services like TendersAlerts offer AI-augmented scraping if you want bulk extraction.

## Payment terms reality

- Government payment terms typically 60–90 days from milestone acceptance.
- Late payment is rare but does happen, particularly in periods of fiscal tightening.
- Cash-flow plan around 90-day delays from acceptance.

## What outside founders should know

- **Most Series A foreign founders win their first KSA government deal via a Saudi prime / SI partner** rather than directly bidding. Plan that route unless you're already RHQ-licensed.
- **Etimad is not search-friendly.** Use sector keywords carefully. The "saved searches" feature is helpful once registered.
- **The unwritten rule:** the most successful first-time government deals come with at least one named pilot champion inside the ministry. Identify them before bidding.
- **Performance bonds and bid bonds** are required for most tenders. Plan for 5–10% bid bond and ~10% performance bond as bank facilities. Some Saudi banks offer faster issuance to foreign-registered suppliers; others require collateral.

## What NOT to do

- Don't try to win Saudi government work without either (a) RHQ or (b) a credible local prime/SI. The system doesn't support it post-Jan 1, 2024.
- Don't price aggressively low to win — Saudi buyers read low prices as signal of low capability or hidden costs.
- Don't promise local manufacturing or transfer-of-tech you can't deliver. ICV claims are audited.
- Don't pay any "facilitation" or "advisory" fee outside of a registered consulting contract — anti-corruption enforcement (Nazaha) is real.
