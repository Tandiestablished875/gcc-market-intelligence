# UAE Government Procurement — Federal vs. Emirate-Level

> Last verified: May 2026. Sources: Federal Authority for Government Human Resources (FAHR), Tamm Abu Dhabi, Dubai Now / DET, MoIAT, ADNOC, DEWA, RTA, ICV certification authorities.

## The fundamental fact

UAE procurement is **fragmented across federal + 7 emirate-level systems**. Unlike Saudi Etimad (one portal), UAE has multiple procurement portals depending on which entity is buying.

Federal procurement and emirate procurement use **different supplier registration systems, different ICV scoring, and different evaluation methodologies.** Knowing which channel applies to your customer is the first step.

## Federal-level procurement

Federal ministries (Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Economy & Tourism, Ministry of Industry & Advanced Technology, Ministry of Foreign Affairs, etc.) procure via:

- **MoF Government Procurement Portal** — centralized federal supplier registration and tender publication.
- Specific ministries also maintain their own pre-qualification lists.
- **ICV certification** — federal-level ICV is operated under MoIAT supervision; scores are reported as percentages.

### How federal ICV works

The **In-Country Value (ICV) Program** assigns each supplier an ICV score based on:
- Local content of goods/services (manufacturing or value-add in UAE).
- Local employment (Emirati + GCC + non-GCC residents).
- Local investment depreciation.
- Revenue earned from local clients.

Suppliers receive an ICV certificate annually from accredited firms (Big 4 + specialized auditors). The certificate is valid 14 months from issuance.

Higher ICV scores receive preference in tender evaluation — typically 10–30% weighting in the overall scoring.

ICV was originally launched by ADNOC (2018) and rolled out as a federal initiative under MoIAT (2021+). It now covers most federal procurement and most major ADNOC, Mubadala, ADQ portfolio company procurement.

## Abu Dhabi emirate procurement

### Tamm (the unified citizen + business platform)

- **URL:** https://www.tamm.abudhabi.
- **Function:** primary Abu Dhabi government services platform. Some procurement and supplier registration happens here; most large tenders also publish on entity-specific portals.

### Key Abu Dhabi procurement entities

- **ADNOC and subsidiaries** (ADNOC Distribution, Logistics, Drilling, Gas, Sour Gas, etc.) — operate their own supplier qualification and tender systems. ADNOC's IKtva-equivalent is the **ICV Program** (which they founded).
- **Department of Health (DoH) Abu Dhabi** — healthcare procurement.
- **Department of Culture & Tourism (DCT)** — culture, tourism, hospitality tech.
- **Department of Municipalities and Transport (DMT)** — smart-city, infrastructure.
- **Abu Dhabi Department of Economic Development (ADDED)** — licensing, economic policy.
- **Etihad Rail** — rail infrastructure tech.
- **Abu Dhabi Distribution Company (ADDC) / TAQA** — utilities.
- **ADQ portfolio companies** — operate their own procurement (Pure Health, Modon, etc.).
- **Mubadala portfolio companies** — operate their own procurement (Strata, EDGE Group, M42, Masdar, etc.).
- **IHC portfolio companies** — operate their own procurement.

### Practical implication

In Abu Dhabi, **each entity has its own procurement system.** Selling to ADNOC and selling to DoH are not the same process — different qualification, different ICV thresholds, different evaluation cycles. Plan per-entity, not per-emirate.

## Dubai emirate procurement

### Dubai Now and DET

- **DET (Department of Economy & Tourism)** — Dubai business and tourism authority; runs the DED system for licensing.
- **Dubai Now** — citizen / business services platform.

### Key Dubai procurement entities

- **DEWA (Dubai Electricity & Water Authority)** — utilities; major buyer for renewable energy, smart-grid, water tech.
- **RTA (Roads & Transport Authority)** — mobility, smart-city transportation, autonomous vehicles, drones.
- **Dubai Health Authority (DHA)** — healthcare procurement.
- **Dubai Municipality** — urban infrastructure, smart-city.
- **Smart Dubai / Dubai Digital** — digital government initiatives.
- **Dubai Police** — security, surveillance, smart-policing tech.
- **Dubai Future Foundation (DFF)** — innovation pilots.
- **Dubai Holding subsidiaries** (TECOM, Jumeirah, Wasl, etc.) — operate their own procurement.
- **ICD portfolio companies** (Emirates Airline, Emirates NBD, ENOC, du) — operate their own procurement.

### Dubai ICV / Local Content

Dubai has historically been less ICV-stringent than Abu Dhabi, but the federal ICV framework increasingly applies. Some Dubai entities (DEWA notably) have their own local content scoring.

## Other emirates

### Sharjah
- Sharjah Government / Sharjah Executive Council procurement.
- **SEDD (Sharjah Economic Development Department)** licensing.
- SEWA (Sharjah Electricity & Water Authority).
- Less centralized than Abu Dhabi or Dubai.

### Ras Al Khaimah
- RAK Government procurement; smaller scale.
- Major industrial procurement via RAK Ceramics, Julphar, etc.

### Ajman, UAQ, Fujairah
- Smaller procurement volumes; entity-by-entity.

## Cross-emirate observations

### What sells across all emirates

- Cloud and AI (with sovereign data-residency)
- Cybersecurity
- Renewable energy and ClimateTech
- Healthtech
- Mobility (especially RTA Dubai, Etihad Rail Abu Dhabi)
- Smart-city / IoT
- Tourism / hospitality tech

### What's emirate-specific

- Defense → Abu Dhabi (EDGE Group, Tawazun)
- Financial services → DIFC (Dubai), ADGM (Abu Dhabi)
- Industrial / heavy manufacturing → Abu Dhabi (KEZAD), RAK
- Aviation / aerospace → Dubai (Emirates), Abu Dhabi (Etihad)
- Ports / maritime → Dubai (DP World), Abu Dhabi (Abu Dhabi Ports), Fujairah

## Practical workflow for a foreign founder

1. **Identify your buyer at entity level.** "Selling to UAE government" is not a strategy. "Selling to DEWA" is.
2. **Check pre-qualification requirements** for that entity. Most require:
   - UAE entity (mainland or free-zone may be acceptable depending on tender).
   - Audited financials (3 years).
   - ICV certificate (where applicable).
   - VAT registration certificate.
   - Performance bonds capability.
   - Sector-specific certifications (e.g. health authority certifications for DoH/DHA).
3. **Identify whether mainland or free-zone matters.** Some emirate-level tenders require mainland LLC (51% Emirati ownership has been waived since 2021 — 100% foreign mainland is allowed in most sectors). Federal tenders are typically more flexible.
4. **Consider Emiratisation.** Mainland 50+ FTE companies face Emiratisation quotas. Free-zone companies are currently exempt.

## ICV scoring in practice

For UAE federal + Abu Dhabi entity tenders that use ICV:

- **Tier 1 supplier** (ICV ≥30%) — strong preference.
- **Tier 2 supplier** (ICV 15–29%).
- **Tier 3 supplier** (ICV <15%) — disadvantaged in evaluation.

To improve ICV score:
- Hire UAE nationals (counts highest).
- Hire long-tenured UAE residents.
- Local manufacturing or local value-add.
- Local supplier procurement.

ICV certification audit firms include the Big 4 (PwC, EY, Deloitte, KPMG) and specialized players.

## Free-zone vs mainland — procurement implications

| Customer type | Mainland needed? | Free-zone OK? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Federal ministry | Usually mainland, some flexibility | Sometimes | Verify per tender |
| Abu Dhabi ADNOC | Mainland typically | Limited | ICV preference matters |
| Abu Dhabi ADQ portfolio | Varies | Varies | Per-portfolio-company |
| Dubai DEWA | Mainland | Limited | |
| Dubai RTA | Mainland | Limited | |
| Dubai DHA | Mainland | Limited | |
| DIFC entity | DIFC LLC fine | DIFC LLC fine | DIFC is a financial free zone |
| ADGM entity | ADGM LLC fine | ADGM LLC fine | ADGM is a financial free zone |
| Sharjah govt | Mainland or Hamriyah/SAIF | Limited | |
| Private family conglomerate | Either fine | Either fine | They care more about reference and quality |

If your buyer is **federal + emirate government**, plan for mainland LLC setup. If your buyer is **private family conglomerate or DIFC/ADGM-listed entity**, free-zone is usually fine.

## What outside founders should know

- **Tamm and Dubai Now are citizen services — not vendor portals.** Vendor registration mostly happens on entity-specific portals (DEWA portal, ADNOC portal, etc.).
- **Federal vs emirate** distinction is the first thing to internalize. Every conversation should start with "which entity?"
- **ICV is becoming more, not less, stringent.** Even for free-zone-based foreign founders, expect ICV-related questions when bidding on federal or major Abu Dhabi procurement.
- **The IPO / public listing track** matters: ADX-listed and DFM-listed entities have additional procurement disclosure requirements and can be slower to onboard new vendors. Pre-listing entities are often faster customers.

## What NOT to do

- Don't assume Dubai and Abu Dhabi procurement work the same way. They don't.
- Don't quote prices without VAT (5%) clearly specified — inclusive vs exclusive matters.
- Don't ignore Emiratisation if you're approaching 50 mainland FTE — fines are AED 9,000/month per shortfall position.
- Don't promise UAE manufacturing for ICV without a clear, costed plan. Audits are real.
- Don't pay any "agent" fee outside a registered services contract. UAE anti-corruption enforcement is rigorous.
